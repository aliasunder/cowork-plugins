# Avignon Breakfast Research — 3-4 Rotating Options (May 25–29)

Great thinking! The hotel breakfast is solid (homemade, 8:00–9:30), but rotating a couple of backup spots during your 5 nights will let you explore the neighbourhood and catch some of Avignon's best pastry scenes. Here are **4 strong options** within 5–10 minutes of the Garlande Hotel (Rue Galante), arranged by vibe and what works for rotation.

---

## Option 1: Chez Lisette — Best Overall, Most Reliable
**What:** Café + boulangerie-pâtisserie hybrid in a charming quiet square
**Where:** Place des Châtaignes (off Rue Armény, ~5-7 min walk from hotel)
**Hours:** 7:30 AM–7 PM (perfect for early-ish breakfasts)
**Speciality:** Pain au chocolat and sacristan pastries (crispy, light, powdered sugar + slivered almonds). All breads and pastries are exceptional.
**For You:** Excellent vegetarian options — pastries, fresh breads, likely quiche or savoury tarts
**For Mom:** This is *her* place. Hand-rolled pastries, coffee, courtyard setting under shade trees. Exactly the aesthetic she loves (rustic, calm, authentic).
**Vibe:** Small quiet square, no tourist crowds. Locals' spot. Table service available.
**Rating:** 3.7–3.8 out of 5 (Tripadvisor/Restaurant Guru) — solid, not flashy
**Cost:** ~€8–12 per person (pastry + coffee)

---

## Option 2: Les Halles d'Avignon (Market Hall) — Graze & Explore
**What:** The iconic covered market you're visiting anyway on Day 2
**Where:** Place Pie, ~5 min walk from hotel
**Hours:** Tue–Fri 6:00 AM–1:30 PM · Sat–Sun 6:00 AM–2:00 PM
**Speciality:** 40+ stalls — pastries, breads, cheeses, charcuterie, prepared food, fresh fruit
**For You:** Olives, artisan cheeses, fresh pastries, vegetable stands. Graze and assemble a light breakfast plate.
**For Mom:** Provençal cheeses (especially Banon — goat cheese in chestnut leaves), fresh croissants, the energy of the market itself
**Vibe:** Busy but manageable in early morning (6–8 AM is quieter). Authentic market energy. Bench seating outside.
**Cost:** €12–18 per person (depending on what you assemble)
**Tip:** Go early (before 8 AM) for smallest crowds and fresh-from-the-oven pastries. This is best as a 1–2x week exploration, not daily.

---

## Option 3: Maison Violette — Convenience + Quality
**What:** Modern boulangerie with fresh pastries and espresso
**Where:** Inside or adjacent to Les Halles / Place Pie area
**Hours:** 7:30 AM–closing (mid-afternoon)
**Speciality:** Croissants, éclairs, fresh lattes. Simple, reliable, quality.
**For You:** Plain croissants or vegetarian pastries, espresso
**For Mom:** Quick, fresh, no fuss. Reliable quality.
**Vibe:** Practical, less atmospheric than Chez Lisette, but faster if you're time-pressed
**Cost:** ~€7–10 per person
**Tip:** Good backup if Chez Lisette is closed or you want minimal fuss.

---

## Option 4: Café Tulipe — Vegetarian Heaven
**What:** Dedicated café with excellent plant-based + veggie options
**Where:** ~7 min walk from hotel (exact address varies; near main square area)
**Hours:** Not confirmed; check on arrival
**Speciality:** Avocado toast, tofu veggie bowls, gazpacho, carrot cake. Plant-based milk options.
**For You:** This is *your* go-to. Everything is designed for vegetarians. Pastries available too.
**For Mom:** Less traditional (she loves pastries + coffee), but nice if she's interested in something lighter.
**Vibe:** Modern, friendly, a bit more touristy
**Cost:** €10–14 per person
**Tip:** Good for one or two mornings if you want something fresher/lighter than pastries.

---

## Why These 4 Spots Work for Your Trip

**Chez Lisette** is your anchor — it's the most "experience" breakfast, walkable, and has that Provençal calm your mom loves. It hits the sweet spot between quality and character.

**Les Halles** is the market you're already visiting on Day 2, so rotation through it for breakfast one morning (ideally Friday, your last day) lets you linger longer, taste-test from different stalls, and really soak in the marketplace experience without rushing.

**Maison Violette** is your practical backup — reliable, quick, adjacent to the market if you need fuss-free.

**Café Tulipe** is your fresh/lighter option if anyone wants a change from pastries (salads, tofu bowls, etc.).

---

## Practical Notes

- **Distance:** All are 5–10 min walk from Garlande Hotel. Flat terrain, walkable with your normal pace.
- **Vegetarian coverage:** Hotel + Chez Lisette + Café Tulipe + Les Halles covers both of you well.
- **Timing:** Hotel breakfast 8:00–9:30 is your anchor each morning. Any alternative breakfast works best as a replace-the-hotel-day, not an add-on.
- **Cost:** Budget €8–18 per person per breakfast for external spots. Hotel breakfast is included, so you're comparing the cost of eating out vs. included morning.
- **Hours:** All are open by 7:30 AM latest, so early birds are covered.

---

## Next Steps

- **Visit Chez Lisette** at least once (Wed is ideal, pre-Palace). Scope it out and see if it feels right for a second visit.
- **Les Halles** on Friday morning (May 29) as your farewell market experience.
- **Skip Maison Violette** unless you need a quick fallback — Chez Lisette and the market cover the rotation nicely.
- **Café Tulipe** is insurance if either of you want something lighter mid-week.
