## Key Research Insights

### Vegetarian Dining in Kyoto
- **Strength:** Yudofu and traditional Buddhist cuisine strongly support strict vegetarians
- **Challenge:** Fish-based dashi is ubiquitous; most casual ramen/soba houses use bonito stock
- **Solution:** Yudofu venues explicitly cater to vegetarians; Michelin venues accommodate with advance notice

### Avoiding Tourist Traps
- **Ruled out:** Ramen Alley (mediocre 3.8/5 rating despite convenience), Pontocho geisha district sushi (inconsistent reviews, premium pricing)
- **Vetted:** Kikunoi and Hyotei maintain 3+ Michelin stars consistently; yudofu establishments are quiet and authentic

### Budget
- Casual options (ramen, soba): ¥900–3,500 (~$7–27 USD)
- Traditional mid-range (yudofu): ¥3,500–6,500 (~$27–50 USD)
- Michelin (kaiseki): ¥12,000–18,000 (~$92–138 USD)
- **Balanced budget:** Mix of yudofu lunches with one Michelin dinner = strong value + memorable experience
