# Tokyo Restaurant Guide

Jordan is vegetarian (strict — no fish sauce, no dashi with bonito). Alex eats everything.

## Day-by-Day Picks (Oct 15–17)

### Oct 15 — Arrival Evening
**Dinner: Ippudo Ramen (casual chain, vegetarian-friendly)**
- **Type:** Ramen shop — comfort meal after travel
- **Why:** Jet lag dinner. Casual, welcoming, no reservation needed. Vegetarian ramen available (miso or tonkotsu broth can be swapped — confirm no bonito).
- **Vegetarian:** Vegetable ramen available; confirm shoyu/tonkotsu base and no bonito
- **Seating:** Counter and tables
- **Location:** Near most central hotels, multiple branches

### Oct 16 — Full Day Activity
**Lunch: Gonpachi Nishi-Azabu (izakaya with mixed options)**
- **Type:** Modern izakaya — casual lunch
- **Why:** Post-morning temple activity. Grill-your-own yakitori experience (Alex) with vegetable skewer options (Jordan). Fun, interactive.
- **Vegetarian:** Vegetable yakitori and tofu grills available
- **Seating:** Tables, casual atmosphere
- **Reservation:** Walk-ups OK for lunch, best time 11:30am–1pm

**Dinner: Tsukiji Outer Market casual (sashimi and teishoku)**
- **Type:** Market-adjacent casual dining
- **Why:** Tokyo seafood experience (Alex). Multiple small restaurants and stalls. Jordan can choose from teishoku (set meal) with grilled fish/tofu/vegetable options.
- **Vegetarian:** Fried vegetable teishoku, tamago (egg) options, vegetable tempura available
- **Seating:** Counter and casual tables
- **Reservation:** Not typically needed
- **Location:** Tsukiji Outer Market

### Oct 17 — Full Day Activity
**Lunch: Ichiran Ramen (casual chain, consistent vegetarian options)**
- **Type:** Tonkotsu ramen
- **Why:** Simple, high-quality lunch. Ichiran is known for flexibility on broth base.
- **Vegetarian:** Custom miso-based broth available; confirm no bonito
- **Seating:** Individual counter booths (privacy)

**Dinner: Nabezo (hotpot restaurant — special experience)**
- **Type:** Sukiyaki/shabu-shabu hotpot
- **Why:** Signature Tokyo experience. Chef-prepared table service. Alex can cook premium beef; Jordan enjoys vegetable, tofu, and mushroom selections in shared broth.
- **Vegetarian:** Vegetarian hotpot menu available (dashi broth with vegetable focus)
- **Seating:** Private tables with table-top hotpot (intimate)
- **Reservation:** REQUIRED (book 2+ days in advance)
- **Location:** Central Tokyo, Ginza area or similar
- **Note:** Confirms non-tourist, interactive dining experience

---

## Research Notes
- Tokyo has abundant vegetarian and vegan options in Shibuya, Harajuku, and central wards.
- Casual ramen and izakaya are forgiving on dietary modifications — direct communication with staff is key.
- Reservation requirements are lighter than Kyoto; walk-ups are feasible most times except dinner rush (6–8pm).
- Hotpot (sukiyaki/shabu-shabu) is excellent compromise dining: shared experience with individual cooking, easy vegetarian adaptation.
- Avoid tourist-heavy Akihabara and Shinjuku for dining; central and residential wards have authentic options.
