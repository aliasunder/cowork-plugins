# Kyoto Restaurant Guide

Jordan is vegetarian (strict — no fish sauce, no dashi with bonito). Alex eats everything.

## Day-by-Day Picks (Oct 18–21)

### Oct 18 — Arrival Evening
**Dinner: Okutan Kappa (traditional yudofu)**
- **Type:** Tofu hot pot (yudofu) — Kyoto specialty
- **Why:** Arrives midday, light dinner makes sense. Yudofu is quintessentially Kyoto and naturally vegetarian-friendly. Casual, warming atmosphere.
- **Vegetarian:** Excellent — core dish is silken tofu in dashi broth with seasonal vegetables
- **Seating:** Communal tables, casual
- **Reservation:** Recommended (walk-ups risky after 6pm)
- **Location:** Kappa Dori area, 10 min walk from central ryokan

### Oct 19 — Full Day Activity
**Lunch: Mishimaen (casual tea house)**
- **Type:** Light lunch near temple (post-Fushimi Inari)
- **Why:** Simple udon or donburi, good rest point mid-sightseeing. Quick turnover, no reservation needed.
- **Vegetarian:** Vegetable udon available; confirm no bonito dashi
- **Seating:** Counter (quick service) or tables

**Dinner: Gion Tanto (kaiseki with vegetarian option)**
- **Type:** Kaiseki — refined, multi-course
- **Why:** Kyoto's signature dining experience. Chef can modify vegetarian tasting if booked in advance. Evening in historic Gion district.
- **Vegetarian:** Call ahead to request vegetarian kaiseki (chef will create custom course)
- **Seating:** Intimate, private room or counter
- **Reservation:** REQUIRED (3+ days advance, email with dietary note)
- **Location:** Gion — atmospheric evening stroll

### Oct 20 — Full Day Activity
**Lunch: Kasagiya (matcha sweet shop + light set meal)**
- **Type:** Casual, matcha-focused
- **Why:** Post-Arashiyama bamboo grove energy boost. Matcha sweets + simple rice bowl.
- **Vegetarian:** Naturally vegetarian
- **Seating:** Casual, walk-ups welcome

**Dinner: Izuju (traditional sushi bar — vegetable/omakase option)**
- **Type:** Sushi restaurant with reputation for flexibility
- **Why:** Kyoto institution. Chef can prepare vegetable nigiri and creative appetizers for Jordan while Alex enjoys sushi selection.
- **Vegetarian:** Request "vegetable sushi course" when booking — chef will prepare creative selection
- **Seating:** Counter (preferred) or tables
- **Reservation:** Recommended
- **Location:** Central Kyoto

### Oct 21 — Departure Morning
**Breakfast:** In-ryokan breakfast (included)
- Typical spread: miso soup, rice, grilled fish/vegetarian option, tsukemono pickles, nori

---

## Research Notes
- Kyoto restaurants book quickly during Oct (peak season). Email or phone 3+ days in advance for kaiseki or special dining.
- Vegetarian options in Kyoto are stronger than Tokyo — tofu traditions, temple cuisine influence.
- Gion and central Kyoto have highest concentration of non-tourist restaurants.
- Yudofu and tofu-based meals are uniquely Kyoto and naturally align with Jordan's diet.
