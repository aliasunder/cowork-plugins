# Kyoto Restaurants — Day-by-Day Picks

## Trip Context
Jordan is vegetarian (strict — no fish sauce, no dashi with bonito). Alex eats everything. 4 nights in Kyoto, Oct 18–21, 2026. Ryokan confirmed (ref KYO-8842) — likely includes breakfast, so picks focus on lunch + dinner.

## Oct 18 (Day 1) — Arrival evening
**Dinner: Shigetsu (Michelin-starred shojin ryori)**
- Address: Shimogyo Ward, Kyoto
- Why: World-class Buddhist vegetarian cuisine — not compromise dining, but the main event. Michelin-starred kaiseki-style shojin ryori. Booking required, seating 6:30 PM.
- Vegetarian: Full tasting menu (Jordan primary choice)
- Alex: Excellent non-vegetarian omakase alternatively available
- Notes: Advance reservation essential (phone or online). Menu changes seasonally.
- Backup: Okutan (tofu-focused, smaller, walk-in friendly)

## Oct 19 (Day 2) — Full day in Kyoto
**Lunch: Nishiki Market food walk (DIY)**
- Why: Self-guided market exploration — pick-as-you-go vegetarian snacks (pickles, tofu samples, mochi, fruit). No reservation needed. Alex samples grilled skewers and fresh seafood.
- Duration: 1–2 hours

**Dinner: Oka (traditional Kyoto-style kaiseki)**
- Address: Shimogawa, near Maruyama Park
- Why: Kaiseki with strong vegetarian accommodation (non-vegetarian omakase also available). Intimate 8-seat counter. Advance reservation required.
- Vegetarian: Full kaiseki adapted by chef
- Alex: Omnivore kaiseki with seasonal seafood
- Notes: Seasonal menu, phone booking recommended
- Backup: Gion Tanto (casual izakaya with vegetable-forward small plates)

## Oct 20 (Day 3) — Full day in Kyoto
**Lunch: Kaiseki Omicho (market-adjacent casual)**
- Address: Shimogyo, near market
- Why: Lighter lunch, vegetarian donburi and soba options. Alex can order seafood-forward donburi. Less formal than dinner reservation spots.
- Duration: 1 hour
- Notes: Walk-in or phone reservation

**Dinner: Gion Tanto (izakaya)**
- Address: Higashiyama Ward, Gion district
- Why: Casual, warm atmosphere. Strong vegetable-forward small plates menu + premium sake list. Alex and Jordan both enjoy exploration-style dining. No reservation required (walk-in possible, but best to book).
- Vegetarian: 8–10 vegetable plates, edamame, yam fries, tofu-skin dishes
- Alex: Yakitori, grilled fish, seasonal preparations
- Notes: Lively but not overwhelming. Counter or table seating.
- Backup: Mizuki (casual soba + tempura)

## Oct 21 (Day 4) — Checkout, likely late
**Brunch/Early lunch: Vermillion Café (casual vegetarian-friendly)**
- Address: Near Fushimi Inari or hotel area (flexible)
- Why: Coffee and light breakfast/brunch before checkout or travel. Vegetarian pastries, vegetable sandwich, yogurt. Alex can order eggs or bacon.
- Duration: 30 min–1 hour
- Notes: Walk-in friendly, no reservation needed

## Vegetarian Dining in Kyoto — General Notes
- Kyoto is one of Japan's most vegetarian-friendly cities due to Buddhist temple culture and shojin ryori tradition.
- Always mention vegetarian requirement when phoning to book (ask for "nashi / no fish stock" explicitly).
- Convenience stores (konbini) have vegetarian onigiri, edamame, fruit, and pastries as backup.
- Seasonal vegetables peak in autumn — many autumn preparations are naturally vegetarian.
