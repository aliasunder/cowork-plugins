# Tokyo Restaurants — Day-by-Day Picks

## Trip Context
Jordan is vegetarian (strict — no fish sauce, no dashi with bonito). Alex eats everything. 3 nights in Tokyo, Oct 15–17, 2026. City arrival/departure — activities light, focus on exploring neighborhoods and dining.

## Oct 15 (Day 1) — Arrival evening
**Dinner: Ichiran Ramen (casual, no reservation)**
- Location: Multiple branches across Tokyo; recommend Shibuya or Shinjuku
- Why: Iconic, efficient ramen experience. Individual seating booths. Alex gets tonkotsu (pork bone broth). Jordan orders vegetable ramen (miso-based, confirm no dashi with bonito). Late dinner okay after travel.
- Vegetarian: Vegetable ramen, tofu, mushroom options available. Ask for vegetable broth explicitly.
- Alex: Tonkotsu or miso ramen with chashu
- Duration: 30 min
- Notes: No reservation, typical wait 10–20 min. Cash preferred (some accept cards).
- Backup: Hanamaru Udon (udon-focused, vegetarian broth available)

## Oct 16 (Day 2) — Full day in Tokyo
**Lunch: Omoide Yokocho market (DIY food exploration)**
- Location: Shinjuku
- Why: Self-guided walk through nostalgic food alley. Grilled yakitori stands, skewers, street snacks. Jordan picks vegetarian options (grilled mushrooms, vegetable skewers). Alex samples grilled chicken, seafood, pork belly.
- Duration: 1.5–2 hours
- Notes: Order as you go, no reservation needed. Cash only mostly.

**Dinner: Kanda (sushi counter, reservation)**
- Location: Ginza or Tsukiji area
- Why: Mid-range sushi experience with skilled chefs. Counter seating, intimate setting. Alex gets premium omakase. Jordan orders a vegetarian alternative (sushi chef can prepare vegetable rolls, tamago, avocado-forward picks).
- Vegetarian: Tamago, cucumber rolls, avocado rolls, vegetable-forward selections — discuss with chef
- Alex: Omakase or à la carte sushi (chef's choice seasonal fish)
- Duration: 1 hour
- Booking: Phone reservation recommended (book 2–3 days ahead)
- Backup: Itamae Sushi (walk-in friendly, casual)

## Oct 17 (Day 3) — Checkout + possible morning activity
**Brunch: Fuglen Coffee (Omotesando or Harajuku branch)**
- Location: Harajuku
- Why: Specialty coffee, light Nordic-inspired brunch. Vegetarian pastries, avocado toast, fresh fruit. Alex orders eggs or bacon. Relaxed atmosphere.
- Duration: 1 hour
- Notes: Walk-in friendly, cash + card accepted
- Alternative: Nearby ramen shop if early departure needed

---

## Tokyo Dining Philosophy
- Ramen, yakitori, and sushi are ramen cultural pilars — exploring all three gives a full picture.
- Vegetarian ramen is becoming increasingly available; always ask for vegetable broth (ask for the kanji 野菜 — yasai).
- Sushi chefs are typically accommodating if you explain dietary restriction in advance.
- Convenience store (konbini) backup: vegetable onigiri, edamame, miso soup, fruit — available 24/7.
- Tipping is not expected in Japan; restaurant prices include service.
