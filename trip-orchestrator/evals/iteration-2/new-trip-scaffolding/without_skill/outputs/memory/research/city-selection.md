# City Selection Research

**Trip:** Japan, October 2026 (10 days)
**Goal:** Identify 2–3 cities that align with priorities: temples, gardens, food, manageable pace (1 main activity/day), accessibility (Alex's knee).

---

## Candidate Cities

### Kyoto (Likely #1 Choice)

**Overview:** Ancient imperial capital, 2000+ temples, Zen gardens, geisha culture, traditional machiya (wooden townhouses). Heart of temple tourism.

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Temples/Shrines** | 2000+. Famous: Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion), Ryoan-ji (zen garden), Fushimi Inari (10k torii gates), Gion temples. |
| **Gardens** | World-class: Ryoan-ji (zen), Kinkaku-ji, Arashiyama bamboo, imperial gardens. Excellent in October. |
| **Food Culture** | Kaiseki capital, shojin ryori (Buddhist vegetarian), local specialties (tofu, yudofu, Kyoto vegetables). Strong vegetarian scene. |
| **Walkability** | Central Kyoto walkable, but old streets have occasional stairs. Taxis recommended for longer distances. |
| **October Condition** | Mild weather, moderate crowds. Too early for peak foliage (November), but gardens still beautiful. |
| **Accessibility (Alex)** | Mixed: central flat areas walkable, but temples often have steps. Taxis available. Elevators in modern hotels. |
| **Days Recommended** | 4–5 days for depth. 3 days minimum. |

**Research needed:** Hotel availability, temple schedules, accessibility details.

---

### Tokyo (Likely #2 or skip)

**Overview:** Modern capital, 1000+ temples, food mecca, museums, nightlife. More urban, more stairs, more crowds.

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Temples/Shrines** | 1000+. Famous: Senso-ji (Asakusa, oldest), Meiji Shrine (peaceful), Tsukiji temples. More scattered than Kyoto. |
| **Gardens** | Good: Meiji Shrine grounds, imperial palace gardens, smaller scattered gardens. Not as impressive as Kyoto. |
| **Food Culture** | Ramen capital, sushi, street food, Michelin-starred restaurants, izakaya. Excellent vegetarian options in Shibuya/Shinjuku. |
| **Walkability** | Very walkable in central areas (Shibuya, Shinjuku, Asakusa). Metro extensive, elevators common. Some stairs in older neighborhoods. |
| **October Condition** | Mild weather, moderate crowds, good conditions. |
| **Accessibility (Alex)** | Good: flat central areas, excellent elevator coverage in metro/buildings. Manageable. |
| **Days Recommended** | 3–4 days. Can combine with day trip (Nikko, Kamakura). |

**Research needed:** Hotel availability, metro accessibility, vegetarian restaurant density.

---

### Osaka (Food Focus)

**Overview:** Japan's food capital, lively street food culture, less touristy than Kyoto/Tokyo. Gateway to Kobe, Koyasan, Himeji.

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Temples/Shrines** | Fewer iconic temples than Kyoto (40+, scattered). Some good options (Sumiyoshi, Isshin-ji), but not core strength. |
| **Gardens** | Limited famous gardens compared to Kyoto. Osaka Castle Park is main garden. |
| **Food Culture** | **Okonomiyaki** (savory pancakes), takoyaki (octopus balls), street food. Vegetarian challenges (often meat-focused). |
| **Walkability** | Dotonbori district walkable, central Osaka walkable. Some stairs, but generally manageable. |
| **October Condition** | Mild weather, good conditions. Less crowded than Kyoto/Tokyo. |
| **Accessibility (Alex)** | Good: relatively flat central areas, metro coverage. |
| **Days Recommended** | 2–3 days (max). Good as hub for day trips. |
| **Day-trip options** | Kobe (~45 min), Himeji Castle (~60 min), Koyasan (~90 min) — Buddhist monastery experience. |

**Research needed:** Day-trip feasibility, vegetarian restaurant options.

---

### Kobe (Smaller, Walkable)

**Overview:** Smaller port city, walkable, less touristy, nearby Arima Onsen (hot springs). Good for slower pace.

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Temples/Shrines** | Moderate: Ikuta Shrine, local temples. Not major draw, but present. |
| **Gardens** | Smaller than Kyoto, but quieter alternatives. Arima region has nature. |
| **Food Culture** | **Kobe beef** (meat-heavy; challenge for you), local seafood, street food. Vegetarian options less abundant. |
| **Walkability** | Very walkable, especially waterfront. Flat, low stairs. Comfortable for Alex. |
| **October Condition** | Mild, good conditions. |
| **Accessibility (Alex)** | Excellent: flat, walkable, few stairs. Best for knee comfort. |
| **Days Recommended** | 3–4 days. Can combine Kobe + Arima Onsen day trip. |
| **Arima Onsen** | Historic hot spring village. Buddhist temple (Arima Cloister). Cultural experience. Escalators/cable car for slope access. |

**Research needed:** Onsen accessibility, vegetarian dining, attraction density.

## Combination Scenarios

### Scenario A: Kyoto + Tokyo (2 cities, deeper)
- **Kyoto:** 5 nights. 1–2 day trips (Nara, Arashiyama if not staying longer).
- **Tokyo:** 4 nights. 1 day trip (Nikko or Kamakura) optional.
- **Pros:** Deep exploration, iconic experiences, good pace.
- **Cons:** Only 2 regions, Tokyo can feel rushed.
- **Best for:** Temple/garden focus with food variety.

### Scenario B: Kyoto + Osaka + Tokyo (3 cities, busier)
- **Kyoto:** 3 nights. Core temples + 1 day trip.
- **Osaka:** 3 nights. Food culture + Koyasan or Kobe day trip.
- **Tokyo:** 3 nights. Core temples + optional day trip.
- **Pros:** Diverse experiences, food variation, regional depth.
- **Cons:** Less time per city, more logistically complex, faster pace.
- **Best for:** Adventurous, want to see everything.

### Scenario C: Kyoto + Kobe/Arima (2 cities, regional, slowest)
- **Kyoto:** 5 nights. Deep temple/garden exploration.
- **Kobe/Arima:** 4 nights. Relaxed pace, onsen, comfort-focused.
- **Pros:** Slowest pace, minimal rushing, Alex's knee most comfortable, less crowded.
- **Cons:** Less variety, fewer iconic temples, fewer food options (Osaka lacks Kobe vegetarian diversity).
- **Best for:** Relaxation, deep Kyoto + cultural rest.

## Recommendation (Pending Confirmation)

**Best fit for your group:** **Scenario A (Kyoto + Tokyo)**
- Kyoto deep-dives temples/gardens (5N satisfies core priority).
- Tokyo adds food dynamism, museums, different urban energy.
- Manageable pace (5 + 4 nights).
- Both cities have mid-range hotels, good metro, decent vegetarian options.
- Accessibility: Kyoto requires taxi backup, Tokyo metro easier — balanced.
- 10 days makes 4–5 hour Shinkansen transfer relaxed (not rushed).

**Alternative if slower pace preferred:** **Scenario C (Kyoto + Kobe)**
- Less ambitious, more restorative.
- Better knee accommodation (Kobe very walkable, few stairs).
- Fewer iconic temples (Kobe weakness).
