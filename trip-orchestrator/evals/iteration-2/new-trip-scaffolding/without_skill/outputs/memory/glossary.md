# Glossary

## Trip-Specific Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **CPP** | Cents per point — measures frequent flyer miles redemption value. Formula: (Cash price / Miles cost) × 100. Target: ≥1.5 cpp for good value. |
| **JR Pass** | Japan Rail Pass — unlimited rail travel on most Japanese railways for 7, 14, or 21 consecutive days. Can be cheaper than point-to-point tickets if traveling extensively. |
| **Shojin ryori** | Buddhist vegetarian temple cuisine — plant-based, intricate, spiritual. Often served at temples or specialty restaurants. Key for vegetarian dining in Japan. |
| **Ryokan** | Traditional Japanese inn with tatami rooms, onsen (hot spring bath), and kaiseki (multi-course) meals. Can be expensive and may have stairs. Vet for accessibility. |
| **Kaiseki** | Multi-course Japanese haute cuisine, often seasonal and art-focused. Typically expensive ($80–200+ per person). Can be vegetarian-adapted. |
| **Izakaya** | Casual Japanese pub/bar, usually serves small plates (gyoza, edamame, grilled items, etc.) with beer or sake. Social, affordable ($15–30 per person). |
| **Tatami** | Woven straw mats used as flooring in traditional Japanese rooms. You remove shoes and sit on the mat (seiza or cross-legged). Can be uncomfortable for extended sitting. |
| **Onsen** | Hot spring bath, often communal and gender-separated. Therapeutic, cultural experience. |
| **Shrine vs. Temple** | **Shrine** = Shinto (indigenous Japanese spirituality, focuses on nature spirits, ancestors). Often has torii gate. **Temple** = Buddhist (religious institution, meditation, statues). Both are culturally significant; temple focus for this trip. |
| **Torii gate** | Orange/red gate marking entrance to Shinto shrine. Iconic symbol. |
| **Yen (¥)** | Japanese currency. ~100 ¥ = $0.70 USD (approximate). October 2026 rate TBD. |
| **Convenience store** | 24/7 shops (7-Eleven, FamilyMart, Lawson) ubiquitous in Japan. Good for snacks, drinks, cash withdrawal (ATM), basic meals. |
| **Suica/Pasmo** | Reloadable IC cards for transit (trains, buses) across Japan. Available at stations, convenient vs. paying cash each time. |

## Dates & Timing

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **October 2026** | Shoulder season in Japan. Mild weather (55–70°F), fewer crowds than summer/spring. Too early for peak autumn colors (November peak), but tail-end foliage and gardens still beautiful. |
| **Peak season** | Cherry blossoms (late March–early April) and autumn foliage (November). Both are crowded and expensive. |
| **School/work schedules** | October typically not a school holiday in USA (unlike spring/summer). Confirm if this affects either planner's availability. |

## Practical Notes

| Topic | Note |
|-------|------|
| **Tipping** | Japan does NOT have a tipping culture. Tipping can be offensive. Bill included with tax. |
| **Cash vs. Card** | Japan is still largely cash-based outside major cities. ATMs at convenience stores accept most international cards. Some temples/gardens accept card. Carry ¥10,000–20,000 in cash at any time. |
| **Toilet facilities** | Public restrooms common in train stations, convenience stores, temples, parks. Toilet paper generally available. Squat toilets still exist in some places (old temples, rural areas). Modern hotels/restaurants have Western-style toilets. |
| **Dress code for temples** | Remove shoes, keep shoulders covered, no revealing clothing. Respectful dress expected. Knees exposed is usually fine. No shoes on tatami mats. |
| **Photography at temples** | Often allowed in main halls, sometimes prohibited in inner sanctums. Ask or look for signs. Some temples charge photo fee. Respect restrictions. |
