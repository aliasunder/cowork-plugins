# Kyoto Restaurants — Day-by-Day Guide

**Trip dates:** Oct 19–23, 2026 (4 days, 3 full days + partial days)
**Context:** Jordan (strict vegetarian, no fish sauce/bonito dashi). Alex eats everything.

---

## Day 1: Saturday, Oct 19 (Arrival evening)

**What's happening:** Arrive in Kyoto evening. Limited time, likely jet-lagged.

### Dinner — Casual, Accessible
**Recommendation: Kyoto Engine Ramen**
- **Why:** Foreigner-friendly, clearly marked dietary options, open 12pm–11pm daily, quick meal
- **Location:** 580-2 Nakanocho, Kawaramachi shopping area
- **Price:** ¥1,200–1,800 ($8–12) per bowl
- **For Jordan:** Vegan soy milk ramen (one of two vegan options)
- **For Alex:** Meat broth ramen, standard or tonkotsu
- **Booking:** Walk-in (no reservation needed)
- **Vibe:** Casual, energetic, good for orientation

**Backup:** Any casual ramen spot within walking distance of hotel if tired.

---

## Day 2: Sunday, Oct 20 (Full day)

**What's happening:** First full day exploring temples/gardens per activity plan.

### Lunch — Temple/Spiritual Experience
**Recommendation: Shigetsu (Tenryuji Temple)**
- **Why:** Aligns with activity rhythm (temple visits). Shojin ryori is profoundly different from Western food. Meditative setting. No compromise needed — Jordan experiences authentic temple vegetarian cuisine, Alex experiences it as traditional Japanese dining.
- **Location:** Tenryuji Temple, Arashiyama (coordinate with temple visit)
- **Price:** ¥8,000–12,000 ($50–75) per person for multi-course fixed menu
- **Booking:** **Reserve in advance** (within a day or two if possible). Phone or website.
- **For Jordan:** Authentic Zen vegetarian cuisine — small dishes, seasonal vegetables, tofu, mushrooms, beautifully composed
- **For Alex:** Multi-course traditional Japanese meal, happens to be vegetarian
- **Vibe:** Quiet, contemplative, garden views. Seated on cushions or low chairs.
- **Duration:** 45min–1 hour

### Dinner — Special, Elegant
**Recommendation: Tousuiro (Gion)**
- **Why:** Iconic Kyoto experience. 130-year-old merchant house. Unique tofu kaiseki. Both diners feel celebrated.
- **Location:** Gion (historic geisha district)
- **Price:** ¥10,000+ ($64+) per person for kaiseki set menu
- **Booking:** **Reserve in advance.** Mention vegetarian/vegan preferences when booking. Website or phone. Advance notice helps kitchen prepare Jordan's vegan version.
- **For Jordan:** Vegan kaiseki menu (all tofu, vegetables, artistic presentation)
- **For Alex:** Tofu kaiseki with potential meat/seafood additions if offered, or full kaiseki as-is
- **Vibe:** Formal, beautiful, traditional wooden machiya setting. International-friendly (Global Vegan Award winner).
- **Duration:** 1.5–2 hours
- **Note:** Premium experience; plan this as the "special dinner" of the Kyoto days.

## Day 3: Monday, Oct 21 (Full day)

**What's happening:** Mid-trip, exploring Fushimi Inari or other activities.

### Lunch — Casual, Local
**Recommendation: Chao Chao Gyoza (Shijo-Kawaramachi)**
- **Why:** Quick, affordable, no-stress option. Clear vegetarian/vegan choices on menu. Good if you want ease after a morning of temple-walking.
- **Location:** Near Kyoto Kawaramachi & Gion-Shijo Stations
- **Price:** ¥1,000–1,500 ($6–9) per person
- **Booking:** Walk-in (no reservation)
- **For Jordan:** Vegetarian gyoza, vegetable sides
- **For Alex:** Meat and vegetable gyoza mix
- **Vibe:** Casual hole-in-the-wall, authentic, fast
- **Duration:** 30–45 min

### Dinner — Contemporary Vegetarian Art Experience
**Recommendation: Uzu (Vegan Ramen)**
- **Why:** Unique, artistic. Darkened room with teamLab projections. Best vegan ramen in Kyoto. Low-key elegant. Different vibe from kaiseki.
- **Price:** ¥1,200–1,800 ($8–12) per bowl
- **Booking:** Walk-in or casual reservation
- **For Jordan:** Two vegan ramen options, both with soymilk-based broths (check for fish-based dashi in background)
- **For Alex:** Likely limited to vegan options on menu; restaurant is vegan-focused. Alternative: pick a different dinner option if Alex wants broader choice.
- **Vibe:** Modern, artistic, communal, quieter/meditative
- **Duration:** 45 min–1 hour

**Alternative Dinner if Alex prefers more variety:**
**Recommendation: Teppan Tavern Tenamonya**
- **Why:** Izakaya grill with vegetarian okonomiyaki, yasai soba, fried potato with cheese + wagyu beef for Alex
- **Location:** Central Kyoto
- **Price:** ¥2,000–4,000 ($12–25) per person
- **For Jordan:** Vegetable-focused dishes (okonomiyaki with veggies, yasai soba)
- **For Alex:** Beef, seafood options + vegetable sides
- **Vibe:** Casual, interactive counter, fun energy

## Day 4: Tuesday, Oct 22–23 (Partial days + departure prep)

**What's happening:** Last full / half-day in Kyoto. Depending on actual schedule and when you leave, choose one meal here.

### Lunch or Early Dinner — Premium Michelin Experience
**Recommendation: Kodaiji Jugyuan** ⭐ Michelin 2-star
- **Why:** The "crown jewel" of Kyoto dining. 100+ years of tradition in Meiji-era building beside temple. Michelin 2-star (2025). High-touch, unforgettable.
- **Location:** Beside Kodaiji Temple
- **Price:** ¥15,000+ ($95+) per person
- **Booking:** **ESSENTIAL: Reserve 1 week in advance.** Phone or website. **Explicitly request vegan-friendly menu** when booking.
- **For Jordan:** Vegan-friendly menu carefully prepared to match the experience
- **For Alex:** Full traditional kaiseki or customized menu
- **Vibe:** Formal, refined, prestigious. Exceptional service and presentation.
- **Duration:** 1.5–2+ hours

**Note:** If timing doesn't work or 1-week advance booking is missed, fall back to **Yuba Seike** (yuba kaiseki, request vegan version; similar experience, slightly less formal).

### Casual Alternative if Timing Awkward
**Recommendation: Veg Out (Kamo River)**
- **Why:** Beautiful river setting, light final meal, scenic. Works if you have limited time.
- **Location:** Facing Kamo River
- **Price:** ¥2,000–4,000 ($12–25) estimated
- **Booking:** Walk-in or casual reservation
- **For Jordan:** Full vegan menu
- **For Alex:** Menu is vegan-focused; may feel limited. Use only as light lunch option, not full dinner.
- **Vibe:** Modern, scenic, casual

## Tips & Notes

**Language & Dietary Communication:**
- Write down or show photos of "no fish sauce," "no bonito dashi," "vegan" on your phone for casual spots
- Premium restaurants (kaiseki) will have English menus or staff who speak English
- Casual ramen shops often have picture menus

**Walkability:**
Given Alex's knee, consider that:
- Gion (Tousuiro): Charming but narrow, uneven streets. OK for one evening walk. Not repeated daily.
- Arashiyama (Shigetsu, Yudofu): Scenic but can be crowded mid-day. Temple itself is serene once inside.
- Casual ramen/gyoza near stations: Very accessible

**Seasonal Note (October):**
October is perfect for Kyoto — pleasant temps, low rain. Most restaurants have outdoor/semi-outdoor seating. No special weather adjustments needed.

**Cancellation Policy:**
When booking advance reservations, confirm free cancellation policy (most restaurants allow 24–48 hour cancellation for free).
