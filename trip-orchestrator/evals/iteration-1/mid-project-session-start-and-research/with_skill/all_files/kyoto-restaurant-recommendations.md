# Kyoto Restaurant Recommendations — Jordan & Alex

**Trip dates:** Oct 18–21 (4 nights, 5 meals)
**Traveler notes:** Jordan (strict vegetarian); Alex (omnivore, eats everything)

---

## The Big Picture

Kyoto has exceptional Buddhist vegetarian cuisine (shojin ryori). This is perfect for Jordan and culturally authentic to Kyoto. Rather than struggling with vegetarian substitutions at traditional kaiseki restaurants, we've identified a world-class shojin ryori strategy: 3–4 temple dinners that showcase refined technique, seasonality, and tradition.

**Strategy:** Anchor dinners around historic temple restaurants. Alex will love the presentation and technique. Jordan gets a fully vegan experience that's iconic to Kyoto.

---

## Top 5 Recommendations (Ranked by Priority)

### 1. Daitokuji Ikkyu (Central Kyoto, Oct 19 dinner)
**What:** Shojin ryori (Buddhist vegetarian)
**Why this night:** Day 2, after arrival recovery. Central location, easy access.
**Details:**
- 500+ years old, oldest shojin ryori in Kyoto
- $80–120 per person
- Multi-course temple meal, fully vegan
- Intimate, historic, quiet atmosphere
- Email in advance; accept walk-in if not booked

**For Alex:** Fine dining without meat focus. Refined vegetable preparation and dashi-free broths. He'll appreciate the technique.

---

### 2. Shigetsu (Arashiyama, Oct 20 dinner)
**What:** Shojin ryori (Buddhist vegetarian, ★★ Michelin)
**Why this night:** Pairs with Oct 20 Arashiyama activity (bamboo grove, temples). Same neighborhood.
**Details:**
- Inside Tenryuji Temple, Arashiyama
- $120–180 per person
- 2-star Michelin, fully vegan, seasonal
- Elegant temple setting, sophisticated plating
- **MUST book in advance** (2–4 weeks)

**For Alex:** Michelin recognition, elevated experience. Arashiyama location creates natural flow from morning/afternoon activities.

---

### 3. Ajiro Honten (Near Myoshin-ji Temple, Oct 21 dinner)
**What:** Shojin ryori (Buddhist vegetarian, ★ Michelin)
**Why this night:** Final dinner, "farewell meal" vibe. Premium option.
**Details:**
- Multi-course Buddhist temple meals
- $90–140 per person
- Longest-holding vegan Michelin star in Kyoto
- Gorgeous presentation, sophisticated technique
- **MUST book in advance**

**For Alex:** Special occasion finale. Michelin-starred vegetarian cuisine—proof that plant-based fine dining is world-class.

---

### 4. Izusen Daiji-in (Daitoku-ji Temple Complex, Oct 19–20 lunch, flexible)
**What:** Shojin ryori (Buddhist vegetarian, lunch-only)
**Why:** Day-of flexibility. Lunch slots better than dinner. Temple setting reinforces morning/afternoon activities.
**Details:**
- Elegant multi-course temple meal
- $70–100 per person
- Lunch only (11am–2pm typical)
- Inside Daitoku-ji Temple compound
- Gorgeous garden views
- Email in advance or call

**For Alex:** Lighter than dinner; still refined. Same cuisine, different time slot for schedule flexibility.

---

### 5. Kanga-an (Near Myoshin-ji, backup option)
**What:** Fucha ryori (Buddhist vegetarian cuisine variant)
**Why:** Backup if top 3 unavailable. Unique five-color symbolic meal.
**Details:**
- $70–110 per person
- Advance reservation required
- Five-color (blue, yellow, red, white, black) presentation = universe symbolism
- Fresh seasonal vegetables
- Intimate, unique experience

## Why Shojin Ryori?

1. **Fully vegan & authentic** — No compromises for Jordan. This is Kyoto's signature vegetarian cuisine.
2. **Culturally iconic** — Zen Buddhist temple tradition, 500+ years old. You can't get this anywhere else.
3. **Fine dining without fish** — Michelin-starred options exist. Elevated experience that doesn't center on seafood.
4. **Alex enjoys it** — Refined presentation, sophisticated technique, unique experience. Not a compromise; it's an upgrade.

**Alternative considered:** Traditional kaiseki at Michelin ★★★ restaurants (Hyotei, Kitcho, Kikunoi). **Rejected** because they're anchored in dashi/bonito stock and fish—vegetarian substitutions would undermine the intended experience. Shojin ryori is the vegetarian-first equivalent.
