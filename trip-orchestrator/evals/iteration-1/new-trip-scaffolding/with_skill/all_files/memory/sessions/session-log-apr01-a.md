## Key Decisions

### Trip Scope
- **Duration:** 10 days (Oct 10–19, 2026 — placeholder, confirm with Alex)
- **Travelers:** Tanisha + Alex (2 people)
- **Cities:** 2-3 cities TBD (Phase 2 decision)
- **Theme:** Depth-focused (3+ nights per city), food-focused, temples, gardens, authentic/less touristy
- **Rationale:** Both travelers want immersion over rushing. Their interests align well with Japan's cultural offerings. Two to three cities allow neighborhood exploration and revisiting favorite spots.

### Budget Framework
- **Total:** $8,000 USD (~$10,880 CAD)
- **Per person:** ~$4,000 USD
- **Estimates:**
  - Flights: $2,000–2,500 (leveraging 85,000 United miles helps significantly)
  - Accommodation: $1,800–2,400 (9 nights @ $200–270/night)
  - Activities: $600–900 (temple/garden entry fees)
  - Restaurants: $2,200–2,800 (food-focused; Japan moderate pricing)
  - Local transport: $300–400 (trains, Suica cards, taxis)
  - Buffer: $500–800
- **Rationale:** This allocates majority spend to experiences (food + activities) while maintaining reserve. Japan is relatively affordable vs. Western Europe. Food is the trip priority, so restaurants get a healthy allocation.

**Alex's Knee:**
- Old soccer injury. Stairs become fatiguing after long activity days (cumulative, not immediate).
- **Planning approach:** Not a hard constraint, but a comfort consideration. Choose hotels with elevators or low floors. Avoid stair-heavy activity chains. Plan rest days naturally.
- **Materials approach:** Frame positively in traveler materials (no medicalization). Focus on good locations and pacing, not accommodation.

**Alex's Language Anxiety:**
- First time in Asia. Nervous about language barrier.
- **Planning approach:** Clear, visual guides. Backup plans flagged explicitly. Reassurance in materials. Photo-rich itinerary.
- **Materials approach:** Provide language sheets with common phrases. Make navigation/logistics very clear. Include emergency numbers.

**Tanisha's Vegetarian Diet:**
- Strict (no meat, poultry, fish).
- Japan has strong vegetarian tradition (plenty of options, not a barrier).
- **Planning approach:** Ensure restaurants have vegetarian options. Cross-check during Phase 6 research. Mention dietary needs when making reservations.
- **Materials approach:** Frame as positive discovery (Japanese vegetarian cuisine) not constraint.
