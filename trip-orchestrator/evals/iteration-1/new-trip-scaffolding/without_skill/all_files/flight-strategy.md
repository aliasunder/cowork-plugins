## Key Facts About United Miles to Japan

### Award Pricing (Off-Peak)
- **Domestic US to Tokyo:** 60,000–70,000 miles round-trip (off-peak)
- **Domestic US to Osaka:** ~70,000 miles round-trip
- **Peak season premium:** Oct is peak (add 20–30K miles for premium pricing)
- **Fuel surcharges:** United awards include fuel on partner airlines, but not all carriers

### Award Value
- **Retail comparison:** $1,200–1,600 for October transcon flights to Japan
- **Miles value:** 85,000 miles = ~$1,275–1,700 in retail, so award redemption is reasonable

## Routing Strategies

### Option 1: Open-Jaw (Recommended for Deep Travel)
- **Example:** SFO → Tokyo (NRT/HND), exit Kansai (KIX = Osaka airport)
- **Cost:** 70,000–85,000 miles round-trip (one-way + segment fees)
- **Advantage:** No backtracking, 10 days fully used for cities, not return travel
- **Question:** Do you have a west coast home base, or elsewhere?

### Option 2: Round-Trip Tokyo
- **Example:** SFO → NRT → SFO
- **Cost:** 60,000–75,000 miles round-trip
- **Advantage:** Simpler logistics
- **Disadvantage:** Must return to Tokyo to fly home (burns 1–2 days, adds train costs)

### Option 3: Round-Trip Osaka
- **Example:** LAX → KIX → LAX
- **Cost:** 65,000–75,000 miles round-trip
- **Advantage:** Closer to Kyoto (1.5hr train), good food city
- **Disadvantage:** Still must return to Osaka

## Partner Airlines to Consider
- **ANA (All Nippon Airways):** Premium partner. No fuel surcharge on awards, excellent service. Standard miles cost.
- **JAL (Japan Airlines):** Good partner, competitive pricing
- **United direct:** Yes, United flies US→Tokyo daily on 787s. No fuel surcharge advantage, but single carrier is simple.
