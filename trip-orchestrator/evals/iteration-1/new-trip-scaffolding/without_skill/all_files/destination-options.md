## Top Considerations for Your Trip

### For temples + gardens:
- **Kyoto** — #1 choice for temples (2,000+) and gardens. Core Japan experience.
- **Kanazawa** — Kenrokuen Garden (top 3 in Japan), fewer tourists than Kyoto, excellent food scene
- **Koyasan** — Buddhist pilgrimage site, mountain temples, unique overnight stays
- **Hiroshima** — Peace Park, quiet temples, excellent nearby garden (Shukkeien)

### For food:
- **Tokyo** — Unmatched diversity, Michelin-starred casual spots, street food culture
- **Osaka** — "Japan's kitchen," casual street food, takoyaki, okonomiyaki, ramen capital
- **Fukuoka** — Minimal tourism, excellent local food, yatai (food stalls)

### For Alex's mobility:
- **Kyoto** — Mixed. Central areas walkable, but temple stairs/hills steep. Afternoon temple visits tough.
- **Kanazawa** — Better. Kenrokuen Garden mostly flat, city less hilly, fewer crowds = less exhaustion
- **Tokyo** — Best for flat walking, excellent public transit, most elevator access in temples/stations
- **Osaka** — Good. Flat central city, excellent transit, food-focused doesn't require long temple hikes

## Common 10-Day Combinations

### Option A: Kyoto + Osaka + Tokyo (Classic)
- **Kyoto:** 4–5 nights (temples, gardens, day trip options)
- **Osaka:** 2–3 nights (food base, nearby Koyasan day trip option)
- **Tokyo:** 2–3 nights (food, energy, modern Japan)
- **Logistics:** Trains easy, well-worn path
- **Alex factor:** Stairs in Kyoto temples = afternoon fatigue risk. Mitigated by rest days.

### Option B: Kyoto + Kanazawa (Deep Travel, Fewer Stairs)
- **Kyoto:** 5–6 nights (temples, gardens, nearby day trips)
- **Kanazawa:** 4–5 nights (Kenrokuen, food scene, less touristy)
- **Logistics:** Shinkansen fast, direct
- **Alex factor:** Better. Kanazawa less hilly, fewer massive temple complexes
- **Trade-off:** Skip Tokyo energy, fewer world-class food spots

### Option C: Tokyo + Osaka + Kanazawa (Modern + Deep Culture)
- **Tokyo:** 3–4 nights (entry, food, modern temples/gardens)
- **Osaka:** 2–3 nights (food hub, central for day trips)
- **Kanazawa:** 3–4 nights (garden, unique culture, pace)
- **Logistics:** Easy shinkansen connections
- **Alex factor:** Best. Tokyo/Osaka flat, fewer demanding temple stairs. Kanazawa is pace-friendly.

### Option D: Kyoto + Tokyo Only (Quality Over Breadth)
- **Kyoto:** 5–6 nights (full temple/garden deep dive)
- **Tokyo:** 4–5 nights (food, neighborhood exploration)
- **Logistics:** Single shinkansen shot, 2-hour train
- **Alex factor:** Mixed. Kyoto temples need strategy, but Tokyo is great
- **Advantage:** Less logistical burden, deeper in each city

## October Weather & Crowds
- **Weather:** Mild (64–73°F), comfortable for walking, occasional rain
- **Crowds:** Post-summer lull, pre-Golden Week. Best season for temples.
- **Advantages:** Temple gardens perfect color, pleasant for long days
- **Disadvantages:** Shorter daylight (6am–5pm), higher prices (peak season)
