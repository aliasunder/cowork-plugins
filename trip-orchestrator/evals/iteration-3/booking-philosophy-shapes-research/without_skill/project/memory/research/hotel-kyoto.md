## Summary

Kyoto offers abundant ryokan options in the 3-4 star, mid-price range that align with your booking philosophy (flexible cancellation, quality, character). The city's two primary neighborhoods for visitors are **Higashiyama** (temple-heavy, traditional, matches Jordan's interests) and **Arashiyama** (scenic, bamboo grove, more touristy). Central Kyoto offers business hotels as fallback but lacks character.

**Top recommendation:** Tier 1 ryokans in Higashiyama (Yumoto or Gion Kappa Zushi), 3–4 nights, for the immersive ryokan experience combined with proximity to temples and gardens. Backup: Mix ryokan nights 1–3 with central hotel night 4 (easier Osaka transfer).

## Vegetarian Dining in Kyoto Ryokans

Kyoto is renowned for Buddhist vegetarian cuisine (shojin ryori), so ryokan vegetarian accommodation is standard—not a special request. Most ryokans in the 3-4 star range:

- Understand vegetarian + vegan dietary needs
- Have vegetarian menus pre-designed for international guests (common enough to justify separate menu)
- Will customize kaiseki dinner to remove fish, bonito dashi, gelatin
- Are familiar with "strict vegetarian" (no fish sauce, no anchovy)

**Best practice:** Email dietary needs 60+ days in advance, include:
- No fish, no seafood
- No bonito dashi (clarify: bonito is a fish used in many Japanese stocks)
- No fish sauce
- No gelatin
- No anchovy
- Optional: "But I eat eggs and dairy. Vegetable, mushroom, and tofu-heavy menus are my preference."

Most Tier 1 ryokans will respond with a custom vegetarian menu tailored to Jordan's needs.
