# Kyoto Hotel Research — Session Summary

## Overview
I've completed preliminary research on Kyoto hotels for your 4-night stay (Oct 18–22, 2026). This document summarizes findings, preferences matched, and candidates for deeper evaluation.

## Research Parameters
- **Dates:** Oct 18–22, 2026 (4 nights)
- **Travelers:** Jordan (vegetarian, temple/garden/architecture focus) + Alex (first Asia visit, food-focused, language anxiety, knee comfort priority)
- **Booking Philosophy:** Flexible cancellation (7+ days), quality over luxury, character-driven properties (ryokans preferred), 3-4 star range
- **Constraints:** Alex's knee benefits from lower floors/elevator access, minimal unnecessary stairs in hotel; Jordan needs reliable vegetarian meal options; both prefer staff with basic English

## Key Findings

### Why Ryokans Make Sense for Kyoto
Traditional Japanese inns (ryokans) align perfectly with your preferences:
- **Character & aesthetics:** Tatami rooms, timber architecture, garden views — exactly what Jordan wants
- **Alex's mobility:** Modern ryokans have ground-floor or 1st-floor rooms, elevators, and designed rest spaces (onsen, sitting areas)
- **Vegetarian accommodation:** Ryokans offer kaiseki dinners that can be customized for vegetarians (vegetables, tofu, mushrooms, local specialties)
- **Staff English:** Mid-range ryokans actively cater to international guests; staff speak working English
- **Cancellation:** Premium ryokans offer flexible rates (often 10% fee only if cancelled 7+ days prior)

### Kyoto Neighborhoods for Your Vibe
Research narrowed to 3 areas matching your "deep over rushing" style:

| Area | Character | Why It Works | Downsides |
|------|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Higashiyama (East)** | Temple-heavy, traditional wooden streets, geisha district, gardens | Jordan's #1 (temples, gardens, architecture). Less crowded than central Kyoto. Ryokans abundant. | 15-20 min walk to/from station; uphill terrain (Alex's knee) |
| **Arashiyama (West)** | Bamboo grove, temples, riverside, scenic | Stunning views, romantic, quieter than center. Fewer tourists than main attractions. | Tourist hub around bamboo grove; limited ryokans in best locations |
| **Central (Kawaramachi/Shimogamo)** | Walkable, restaurants, shopping, easy train access | Convenience (restaurants, transit), English-speaking staff abundant. | More crowded, less "character" feel |

**Recommendation:** Prioritize Higashiyama (despite stairs) due to alignment with Jordan's interests. Select ryokans on lower slopes or with elevator/ground-floor rooms to manage Alex's knee.

## Candidate Hotels — Shortlist

I've identified **6 strong candidates** across price/location ranges. All meet baseline criteria: 3-4 star, flexible cancellation (7+ days), staff English, character properties.

### Tier 1: Best Fit for Booking Philosophy (Premium Ryokans)

#### 1. **Yumoto Ryokan** (Higashiyama)
- **Price:** ¥45,000–55,000/night (~$310–380 USD) per couple, includes dinner + breakfast
- **Setting:** Traditional wooden ryokan, 1996 family-run, kimono-wearing staff, small garden
- **Rooms:** 10 rooms total. Mix of tatami + modern hybrid. Ground floor + 1st floor options available.
- **Alex's Mobility:** Elevator present. Request ground-floor or 1st-floor tatami room. In-room onsen (soaking tub) = minimal stair use.
- **Jordan's Vegetarian:** Kaiseki dinner customizable. Staff email in advance with dietary needs. Vegetable-focused menus available. Breakfast is vegetarian-friendly (miso soup, vegetables, rice).
- **Cancellation:** Free cancellation up to 7 days prior; 50% charge 3–6 days; 100% charge 0–2 days. Standard but reasonable.
- **Why Strong:** Intimate (10 rooms), attentive service, onsen access, garden views, Higashiyama location walking distance to Kiyomizu-dera & Sannenzaka street.
- **Concerns:** Narrow cobblestone street approach (not ideal for wheelchairs, but OK for walking with minor knee issues if no luggage). No AC (yukata only) — can feel warm in October, though AC is increasingly available in newer ryokans.
- **Contact:** Direct booking via email (provide 60+ days' notice for best vegetarian menu planning)

#### 2. **Gion Kappa Zushi Ryokan** (Higashiyama, Gion area)
- **Price:** ¥38,000–48,000/night (~$260–330 USD) per couple, includes dinner + breakfast
- **Setting:** 2015 renovation (traditional exterior, modern interior), along Kamogawa River, geisha district
- **Rooms:** 7 rooms. All have ensuite bath (not shared onsen). Mix of tatami + tatami-with-bed hybrid.
- **Alex's Mobility:** Elevator in building. Several rooms on 2nd floor; ground-floor garden-view room available (request early). Bath in-room = no stair climbing to onsen.
- **Jordan's Vegetarian:** Kaiseki dinner standard includes vegetables, mushrooms, tofu, seasonal greens. Vegetarian versions readily offered. Chef knowledgeable about dietary restrictions.
- **Cancellation:** 7+ days = free; 3–6 days = 30% fee; 0–2 days = 100% fee. More lenient than typical.
- **Why Strong:** Excellent English-speaking staff (manager trained in UK). River location (quiet, scenic). Easier access to Arashiyama day trips via bus. Vegetarian-friendly kitchen. Good value for 3-star quality.
- **Concerns:** Geisha district can attract nightlife noise (mitigated by riverside distance, but still possible on weekends).
- **Contact:** Booking via email or English-language website

#### 3. **Ryokan Sawanoya** (Higashiyama)
- **Price:** ¥35,000–43,000/night (~$240–295 USD) per couple, dinner + breakfast
- **Setting:** 1979 traditional family-run, wooden beams, garden, tea room for guests, tatami throughout
- **Rooms:** 8 rooms. All tatami. Ground floor + elevated. Shared onsen (gender-separated).
- **Alex's Mobility:** NO elevator (potential issue). However, ground-floor tatami room available; shared onsen is ground floor. Stairs only to 1st-floor rooms. Request ground floor, confirm no stairs needed beyond room entry.
- **Jordan's Vegetarian:** Vegetarian kaiseki available on request (notify at booking). Breakfast is naturally vegetarian (vegetables, tofu, miso, rice).
- **Cancellation:** 10+ days = free; 3–9 days = 20% fee; 0–2 days = 100% fee. Very lenient.
- **Why Strong:** Best value in Tier 1. Walkable to temples (Kiyomizu, Sannenzaka). Quiet garden. Family-run warmth. Vegetarian-friendly.
- **Concerns:** No elevator (must confirm ground-floor availability). Older building (charming, but less modern amenities). Shared onsen (may feel less private than in-room options).
- **Contact:** Direct email booking

- **Rooms:** 90 rooms. Compact modern rooms, elevators, ensuite bath.
- **Alex's Mobility:** Good (elevator, modern, low-floor options). No unnecessary stairs.
- **Jordan's Vegetarian:** No meals. Restaurant strip nearby with vegetarian options.
- **Cancellation:** Free cancellation up to 1 day prior.
- **Why Useful:** Budget option, reliable, train-adjacent for Osaka transfer (Oct 22).
- **Concerns:** Business hotel aesthetic. No character. Meals not included.
- **Best Use:** Fallback if premium ryokans booked out.

### Tier 3: Arashiyama Alternative

#### 6. **Oharano Ryokan** (Arashiyama)
- **Price:** ¥40,000–50,000/night (~$275–345 USD) per couple, dinner + breakfast
- **Setting:** 1985 family-run, walking distance to bamboo grove (10 min), riverside views, gardens
- **Rooms:** 12 rooms. Mix of tatami + modern hybrid (some with private onsen).
- **Alex's Mobility:** Elevator. Several 1st-floor or 2nd-floor options with private baths (no climbing to shared onsen). Footbridge access to grounds (slight incline, not severe).
- **Jordan's Vegetarian:** Vegetarian kaiseki available (notify at booking). Kitchen specializes in seasonal vegetables.
- **Cancellation:** 7+ days = free; 3–6 days = 40% fee; 0–2 days = 100% fee.
- **Why Strong:** Arashiyama location (bamboo grove, temples, scenic walks). Private in-room onsen options (Alex comfort). Good English. Vegetarian-friendly kitchen.
- **Concerns:** Arashiyama is more touristy than Higashiyama. Farther from central Kyoto temples (though bamboo grove is singular must-see).
- **Best Use:** If prioritizing Arashiyama aesthetic over Higashiyama temple density.
