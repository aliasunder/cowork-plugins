# Response to Mom's Cobblestone Question

**Q: "Can mom handle cobblestones? We'll be walking around Avignon old town."**

---

**Short answer: Yes.** Cobblestones are totally manageable for her, especially in Avignon where the old town is compact and walkable at a leisurely pace.

---

## Why this works for mom

The real constraint with her arthritis isn't *type* of terrain — it's **volume and intensity**. Her feet and back get sore after prolonged standing or walking, but short, leisurely strolls with natural rest breaks are exactly what you've already planned.

Avignon's cobblestoned streets are:
- **Flat** (no steep hills in the pedestrian core)
- **Compact** (everything is 5–10 min walk from the hotel)
- **Built for stopping** (cafés, benches, Place de l'Horloge as a natural gathering point)
- **Authentic at a slow pace** (wandering, not rushing)

This is actually ideal. The cobblestones *add character* — they're not a problem if you're moving at a stroll with breaks.

---

## Practical notes

- **Rue des Teinturiers** (the canal street with waterwheels) is the main cobblestoned walk you'll do — it's beautiful, flat, ~8 min from the hotel, and lined with cafés for stopping. It's perfect for mom.
- **Market day** (Les Halles) is indoors on flat floors — no cobblestone concerns.
- **Rest days and long lunches** are already baked into the schedule, so her feet and back get recovery time.
- **Shoes matter more than terrain** — make sure she packs comfortable, supportive walking shoes with good grip (not dress shoes).

---

## What to avoid (not specific to Avignon)

Stacking multiple physical activities back-to-back (e.g., Palais des Papes stairs + a long walk the same day) is what causes cumulative strain. That's why the itinerary has the Palace as the main event on Palace day, with full rest after. One main activity per day, with long rest periods between.

Cobblestones themselves? Not a problem.

---

## Bottom line

Plan the walks as you normally would — the old town, the canal street, the squares. Just keep the rhythm you've already planned (morning activity, long lunch, afternoon rest, optional evening stroll). Mom will be fine.
