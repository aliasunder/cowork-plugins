# Can Mom Handle Cobblestones? Avignon Old Town

Yes, mom can handle cobblestones in Avignon's old town, but with some important context about what to expect and how to approach them.

## The Good News

From the destination guide, **Avignon's intra-muros (the walled old town) is "mostly flat with some gentle slopes" and has "cobblestones in places but generally smooth stone paving."** This is actually one of Avignon's accessibility strengths compared to other European medieval cities.

The key distinction: some streets have proper cobblestones (like Rue des Teinturiers, the atmospheric canal street), but many of the main walking routes through the old town use smooth stone paving rather than the uneven, ankle-twisting cobblestones you might see in other places.

## What You Need to Know About Mom's Feet

Mom has **arthritis in her toes with significant pain after prolonged standing/walking**. Her comfort (not disability) is what matters. She can do stairs and uneven terrain, but cumulative strain is the real concern — avoid stacking stairs + long walks + standing in the same outing.

## Strategy for Avignon

1. **Wear proper shoes.** The destination guide emphasizes: "Cobblestones in Avignon and Verona, stone floors in churches, and Venice's smooth-but-sometimes-slippery fondamenta all demand flat, sturdy shoes with good grip — not sandals, not flip-flops." This is non-negotiable for mom. Break her shoes in before the trip.

2. **Plan the pace.** Most of Avignon's old town is accessible with deliberate movement. You're not doing long trekking — you're strolling between cafés, the market, the Palace, and resting spots. The itinerary builds in frequent breaks (rest at the apartment, long sit-down meals, 2–3 hour rest periods after major activities).

3. **Watch the specific activities:**
   - **Les Halles market** (May 26): Indoors, flat, air-conditioned. Benches outside. Very manageable.
   - **Rue des Teinturiers** (May 26 afternoon): Cobblestones but flat, lined with cafés. Plenty of places to sit and slow down.
   - **Palais des Papes** (May 27): The big physical day — "significant stairs inside" and "uneven stone floors." This is THE activity where mom will feel the cumulative strain. That's why the itinerary gives her a 3-hour rest period afterward and keeps it as the only major physical activity that day.
   - **À La Française day trip** (May 28): Involves walking through villages and along a river path. Fontaine-de-Vaucluse has a note: "Walk to wherever it feels comfortable, then turn back. The river and the turquoise water are beautiful well before the source. Don't push for the top."

## Bottom Line

Cobblestones aren't a barrier for Avignon — mom can absolutely handle them. The real management is:
- Good shoes (critical)
- Spacing out physical activities with rest periods
- Knowing it's okay to walk part of a route and stop, rather than completing every path
- Accepting that the Palace visit (May 27) is the strenuous day, and planning everything else around recovery

Avignon is actually your most manageable medieval city on this trip for someone managing foot pain. The town isn't steep, isn't sprawling, and the itinerary you've built already accounts for her comfort.
