# Trip Planning Approach — Thinking & Methodology

**Session:** March 31, 2026 — Japan October Trip Planning Scaffold
**User Request:** Help plan a 10-day Japan trip for you and Alex (partner, bad knee, first time to Asia, vegetarian/omnivore, food/temples/gardens focused, $8K budget + 85K United miles)

## My Thinking Process

### 1. Initial Information Intake
**What I heard:**
- 10-day October trip to Japan (first time for both)
- $8,000 USD budget + 85,000 United miles
- Two travelers with different dietary needs and one mobility constraint
- Clear priorities: food, temples, gardens, depth over breadth
- Language barrier anxiety from Alex
- Mid-range experience level (not backpackers, not luxury travelers)

**Red flags identified:**
- Alex's knee: "stairs are rough after a long day" → Not disabled, but real practical constraint. Need to plan itineraries that minimize stair fatigue in afternoons/evenings.
- First time to Asia: Need language support, clear guidance, not overwhelming logistics
- Vegetarian planner + omnivore partner: Both need valid restaurant options (not "here are 5 vegetarian restaurants"; instead "here's what you eat, here's what Alex eats")
- October is peak season: Flights will be expensive (in miles), hotels pricier, temples crowded

### 2. Budget Analysis

**Math:**
- $8,000 cash budget + 85,000 United miles
- 85,000 miles = roughly $1,300–1,700 in retail flights (depends on routing/cabin)
- That leaves $8,000 for everything else

**Rough allocation:**
- Flights: $300 cash (taxes/fees; rest in miles)
- Hotels: $1,500 (10 nights @ $150/night, mid-range)
- Food: $500 (core priority, $40–60/day with some splurges)
- Activities: $200 (temples, gardens)
- Transport: $300 (trains between cities, airport, local transit)
- Contingency: $700 (exchange fees, tips, surprises)
- **Total: $3,500 cash needed**
- **Remaining: $4,500 buffer** (significant flexibility)

**Conclusion:** Budget is realistic and generous. No real constraints unless you choose luxury hotels/fine dining exclusively.

### 3. City Selection Framework

**Key criteria for YOUR trip:**
1. **Temples + gardens** (must-haves)
2. **Food culture** (core priority)
3. **Alex's mobility** (stairs matter)
4. **Deep travel** (2–3 cities, not rushing)
5. **First-time Asia experience** (need walkability, transit clarity, less overwhelming)

**City evaluation:**
- **Kyoto:** #1 for temples (2,000+) and gardens, BUT hilly, lots of stairs, intense temple tourism. Needs strategy for Alex.
- **Tokyo:** Best for food diversity, flat, excellent transit, modern temples with elevators, BUT massive and potentially overwhelming for first-time Asia visitors
- **Osaka:** Food culture ("Japan's kitchen"), relatively flat, excellent transit, less touristy than Kyoto, good base
- **Kanazawa:** Kenrokuen garden (top 3 in Japan), less crowded, good food, less hilly, fewer monumental temple complexes = less stair fatigue for Alex
- **Koyasan:** Unique mountain temple experience, but very stair-heavy, not ideal for Alex
- **Hiroshima:** Quiet, respectful, good gardens, but less food-focused

**My recommendation framework:**
- **Option A (Classic):** Kyoto + Osaka + Tokyo (well-worn path, pros: temples + food + energy, cons: three cities = rushing, Kyoto stairs)
- **Option B (Deep + Smart):** Kyoto + Kanazawa (temples + gardens + food, fewer stairs, less touristy)
- **Option C (Balanced):** Tokyo + Osaka + Kanazawa (modern + food hub + quality culture, best for Alex's mobility)
- **Option D (Focused):** Kyoto + Tokyo (depth in two cities, but Kyoto stairs need strategy)

### 4. Flight Strategy

**Your constraints:**
- 85,000 United miles available
- October (peak season = higher award pricing)
- Likely departing from US west coast (assumed, but didn't presume)
- 10 days = want to maximize time in destination

**Award pricing reality:**
- Off-peak US→Tokyo: 60,000–70,000 miles
- Peak October: 75,000–85,000+ miles
- You likely need most/all of your 85,000 miles

**Routing recommendation:**
- **Open-jaw is better than round-trip** (e.g., fly into Tokyo, exit Osaka) — saves 1–2 days of return travel, maximizes time in destinations
- **Partner airline benefit:** ANA (All Nippon) has no fuel surcharge on awards, makes sense to check ANA awards on United portal

**Next: Await your cities + dates decision, then check United.com for availability**

### 5. Accommodation Philosophy

**Your stated preferences:** Mid-range hotels with character, NOT Airbnb, 3–4 star
**Alex's constraint:** Elevator OR ground/1st/2nd floor

**Why this matters:**
- Japanese older hotels often have stairs
- Temples = stairs everywhere, can't avoid
- Solution: Base hotel in walkable neighborhood with elevator, plan temple visits strategically (mornings when fresh, not after 3pm hikes)

**Approach:**
- Shortlist 3–4 options per city
- Verify elevator in booking description
- Check Booking.com/Google reviews for stair mentions
- Prefer central, walkable neighborhoods to minimize ground transit

### 6. Dining Strategy

**Key insight:** You're vegetarian, Alex eats everything
- **Don't frame restaurants as "vegetarian-friendly" and "omnivore"**
- **Instead:** Present day-by-day with "Alex's pick" (meat focus) and "Your options at same restaurant" (vegetarian)
- **Recognition:** Alex should be primary audience for restaurant research (he eats more variety); your vegetarian needs are integrated, not separated

**Japanese food context:**
- Vegetarian options harder than Europe (less casual veggie restaurants, more fish broth in "vegetable" dishes)
- BUT: Quality vegetarian cuisine exists (Buddhist temple cooking = shojin ryori)
- Solution: Mix casual spots (ramen with vegetable miso broth, udon, soba) with intentional vegetarian discoveries

### 7. Language Barrier Mitigation

**Alex's concern:** "Nervous about language barrier"

**Practical solutions:**
1. **Google Translate app:** Download Japanese offline, use camera translation for menus
2. **Pocket phrase guide:** Simple printable (hello, thank you, excuse me, help, station names)
3. **Pre-book restaurants:** Reduce day-of ordering anxiety, confirms availability
4. **Suica IC card:** Tap and go on transit, no conversation needed
5. **Tourist info centers:** Staff speak English in major cities
6. **Translation earbuds:** Real-time translation (Pixel Buds, AirPods Pro) if you want high-tech help

**Deliverable for next session:** Simple language guide (20–30 essential phrases) in French + Italian style

### 8. Itinerary Pacing for Alex's Mobility

**Key principle:** Stairs compound fatigue. Morning fresh ≠ evening tired.

**Suggested approach:**
- **Morning:** Temple visit (lots of stairs, fresh energy)
- **Midday:** Casual lunch, walk around neighborhood (flat areas)
- **Afternoon:** Garden visit (mostly flat, peaceful)
- **Evening:** Restaurant dinner (seated, rested)
- **One full rest day per city** (no temples, just neighborhood exploration, food)

**Example Kyoto day:**
- 7:30am: Fushimi Inari (stairs, but mostly flat at bottom)
- 10:00am: Breakfast
- 11:00am: Walk riverside (flat)
- 1:00pm: Lunch
- 3:00pm: Ryoan-ji Garden (flat, contemplative)
- 6:00pm: Dinner
- 8:00pm: Hotel rest
