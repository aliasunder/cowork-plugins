## Key Insights from Research

1. **Kyoto's vegetarian dining is exceptional** — Buddhist temple cuisine (shojin ryori) is world-class and fully vegan. No compromises needed.

2. **Traditional kaiseki is fish-centric** — Michelin ★★★ options (Hyotei, Kitcho, Kikunoi) rely heavily on dashi/bonito. Vegetarian substitutions compromise the intended experience. Rejected in favor of shojin ryori.

3. **October booking lead time is critical** — Michelin-starred and temple restaurants fill 2–4 weeks out. Delaying bookings beyond early April risks limited availability.

4. **Temple restaurants offer unique logistics** — Inside temple compounds (Tenryuji, Daitoku-ji) with peaceful settings. Can combine dining with temple activities.

5. **Alex's omnivore status is asset** — He can experience refinement of plant-based haute cuisine (shojin ryori) without missing meat. The technique and presentation matter more than the ingredient.
