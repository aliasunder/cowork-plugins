## Session Work: Kyoto Restaurant Research

### Methodology Applied

Followed **Trip Orchestrator skill's research-methodology reference** with 6-phase process:

1. **Phase 1: Scope & Criteria**
   - Defined hard filters: vegetarian accommodation for Jordan, advance bookings, recent reviews
   - Identified nice-to-haves: traditional Kyoto cuisine, shojin ryori, character/ambiance
   - Set budget range: $50–150 USD per meal

2. **Phase 2: Broad Discovery**
   - Cast wide net across Michelin Guide, Tabelog, TripAdvisor, Google Maps, local food blogs
   - Compiled 20+ initial candidates

3. **Phase 3: Cross-Validation**
   - Checked ratings across 2–3 sources for consistency
   - Verified vegetarian accommodation specifics for each option
   - Reviewed recent reviews (past 6–12 months)
   - Located restaurants relative to planned activities

4. **Phase 4: Structured Comparison**
   - Built comparison table with 18 restaurants organized by price tier
   - Columns: Name, Cuisine, Rating, Vegetarian Accommodation, Price, Booking Method, Notes

5. **Phase 5: Ruled-Out Table**
   - Documented rejections with rationale
   - Eliminated traditional kaiseki (fish-heavy), tourist chains, high-traffic tourist areas

6. **Phase 6: Recommendation**
   - Produced shortlist with day-by-day slot recommendations
   - Presented top picks, runners-up, backup options in schedule context
   - Flagged booking urgency and verification needs

### Research Findings

**Critical discovery:** Kyoto has exceptional vegetarian dining infrastructure, particularly shojin ryori (Buddhist Zen temple cuisine). This is uniquely aligned with Jordan's strict vegetarian needs and offers an culturally authentic Kyoto experience unavailable in other cities.

**Top Options Identified:**

**Shojin Ryori (Buddhist Vegetarian Temple Cuisine) — Recommended Primary Strategy:**
- **Shigetsu** (Tenryuji Temple, Arashiyama) — ★★ Michelin, $120–180, fully vegan, seasonal
- **Daitokuji Ikkyu** (Daitoku-ji Temple, central) — 500+ years old, $80–120, fully vegan, historic
- **Ajiro Honten** (Myoshin-ji area) — ★ Michelin (longest-holding vegan star), $90–140, fully vegan
- **Izusen Daiji-in** (Daitoku-ji Temple) — Lunch-only, $70–100, fully vegan, multi-course temple meals
- **Kanga-an** (near Myoshin-ji) — Fucha ryori variant, $70–110, fully vegan, five-color symbolic meal

**Ruled Out — Traditional Kaiseki (Michelin ★★★):**
- Hyotei, Kitcho, Kikunoi, Nishikawa, Gion Maruyama
- **Reason:** Fish-heavy (dashi, bonito stock). Jordan would miss the intended experience. Vegetarian substitutions compromise the cuisine's integrity. Shojin ryori is the vegetarian-first Kyoto experience.

**Casual Options (Walk-in, no reservation):**
- Veg Out (vegan café), Uzu (vegan ramen with teamLab art), Ramen Towzen (soy ramen), Mumokuteki Café (vegetarian)

   - **Impact:** Unlocks 5 high-quality options, all deeply authentic
