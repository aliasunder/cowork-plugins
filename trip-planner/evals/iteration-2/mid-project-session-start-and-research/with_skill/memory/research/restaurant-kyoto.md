## Phase 3: Cross-Validation

### Shigetsu (Tenryu-ji)
- **Sources:** Michelin Guide (1-star), official Tenryu-ji website, TripAdvisor (multiple 5-star reviews), OMAKASE JapanEatinerary, Yelp (4.8), Inside Kyoto
- **Consensus:** Consistently praised as Kyoto's finest shojin ryori, UNESCO site, intimate temple garden setting
- **Verification:** Hours confirmed 11am–2pm lunch only (no dinner). Reservation required 3+ days advance. Phone: (075) 882-9725. Garden fee ¥500 additional.
- **Dietary Match:** 100% plant-based, entirely suitable for Jordan. Alex will enjoy the artistry and uniqueness.
- **Risk:** Peak season (Oct), book ASAP. No evening option (lunch only).

### Omen (Udon near Ginkaku-ji)
- **Sources:** Inside Kyoto (recommended), TripAdvisor (4.8+), traditional food guides, Lonely Planet
- **Consensus:** Kyoto's best udon, local favorite, seasonal menu
- **Verification:** Walk-in, expect crowds Oct afternoons (after Fushimi Inari visit). No reservation system mentioned.
- **Dietary Match:** Hot/cold broths available; vegetarian options clear.
- **Risk:** Can be crowded; arrive early lunch or expect wait.

### Gion Maruyama (Kaiseki)
- **Sources:** Michelin Guide (2-star), premium dining guides, OMAKASE JapanEatinerary
- **Consensus:** Highest-rated kaiseki in Kyoto, traditional, Michelin recognized
- **Verification:** Requires phone reservation 1–2 weeks in advance. Vegetarian option available (must note dietary restrictions when booking).
- **Dietary Match:** Jordan's dietary needs require special request; standard practice for kaiseki.
- **Risk:** Expensive (~$150–200/person), needs early booking.

### Uzu (Vegan Ramen)
- **Sources:** Web search highlights as "best vegan ramen," recent reviews (4.8), plant-based travel guides
- **Consensus:** Specifically designed for vegan/vegetarian diners, unique teamlab experience
- **Verification:** Downtown Kyoto, accepts walk-ins and reservations. Must confirm hours and location.
- **Dietary Match:** 100% vegan broth and toppings, ideal for Jordan's strictness.
- **Risk:** Specialized venue; verify it's still operating and location.

### Hinode Udon (Near Nanzen-ji)
- **Sources:** Inside Kyoto, udon guides, Tabelog (local site)
- **Consensus:** Casual, walkable from Nanzen-ji activity
- **Verification:** No specific hours found; assume lunch-focused (typical udon).
- **Dietary Match:** Vegetarian broths available.
- **Risk:** May close early afternoon; phone ahead to confirm hours.

**Morning Activity:** Fushimi Inari (7am early start, ~2.5–3 hrs)
**Arrival Time at Ryokan:** ~10:30am–11am
**Suggested Meals:**

3. **Dinner (6–7pm, settle in at ryokan):** Casual, relaxing

## October Weather + Restaurant Notes

October 15–25 in Kyoto: 57–71°F (14–23°C), mostly sunny, occasional rain early in month. By late Oct, autumn leaves begin. Dress in layers.

- Early 7am breakfasts: Cool mornings, warm tea/soup options comfortable
- Lunch (noon): Comfortable, lighter meals suitable
- Dinner: Cool evenings, warm broth dishes (ramen, noodles) appreciated
- Shigetsu garden setting: Beautiful autumn light, cool air — dress warmly for outdoor garden views
