## Strategic Decisions

### Trip Philosophy
- **Not a sprint** — 10 days, 2–3 cities means ~3–5 days per city. Unhurried. One main activity per day.
- **First Asia trip** — Both of you first-time travelers to Asia. Need support (language phrasebook, basic orientation) but not hand-holding.
- **Constraints are features** — Alex's knee + language anxiety aren't trip-killers; they shape a slower, more manageable pace that actually improves experience depth.
- **Food is integral** — Not a side activity; restaurants and street food are primary cultural engagement. Both of you love food.

### City Selection (TBD)
**Likely candidates:**
- **Kyoto** — #1 choice likely. 2000+ temples, Zen gardens, traditional machiya, walkable central core, fall foliage (November peak, October tail). Lower stairs than Tokyo.
- **Tokyo** — Major hub, food mecca, museums. Higher density, more stairs, potentially more overwhelming. Good for food + modern culture.
- **Osaka** — Food capital, day-trip hub, less touristy than Kyoto/Tokyo. Gateway to Kobe/Koyasan.
- **Kobe** — Smaller, walkable, Arima Onsen nearby (temple culture), food. Less crowded.

**Rough scenario (needs confirmation):**
- **Option A:** Kyoto (4N) → Tokyo (4N) + day trip → Return home. (2 cities, deeper)
- **Option B:** Kyoto (3N) → Osaka (3N) → Tokyo (3N) + day trip. (3 cities, busier)
- **Option C:** Kyoto (5N) → Kobe/Arima (3N) → Return. (Stay regional, very deep)

### Activities

**Priorities (in order):**
1. **Temples** — Buddhist and Shinto sites. Kyoto is epicenter.
2. **Gardens** — Zen gardens, stroll gardens. Kinkaku-ji, Ryoan-ji, Katsura Imperial Villa examples.
3. **Food culture** — Market tours, street food, cooking classes (if time/energy permits).
4. **Museums** — Secondary. Include only if rainy-day alternative or strong interest.

**October specifics:**
- **Weather:** Mild, 55–70°F (13–21°C). Occasional rain. Layers + light rain jacket.
- **Foliage:** Too early for peak autumn colors (November peak), but tail-end flowers and green gardens are still beautiful.
- **Crowds:** Shoulder season — fewer tourists than summer/spring, but more than winter.

**Activity pace:** 1 main activity per day. Examples:
- Day 1: Arrive, explore hotel neighborhood, light meal.
- Day 2: Major temple complex (e.g., Kinkaku-ji, spend 2–3 hours).
- Day 3: Garden day (e.g., Ryoan-ji, Arashiyama).
- Day 4: Market/food-focused or museum.
- Day 5: Day trip or rest day with casual walks.
