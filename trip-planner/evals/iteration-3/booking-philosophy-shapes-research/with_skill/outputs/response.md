You've prioritized ryokans and traditional properties with character, and your booking philosophy (flexible/refundable, willing to pay for comfort) opens up higher-end options that smaller budget travelers skip. I focused on:

## The Shortlist (in order of fit)

### Best Match: Ryokan Yumoto
**What it is:** Traditional ryokan in Higashiyama, 17 rooms, authentic but modern-accessible.

**Why it's top:**
- Full ryokan experience (tatami, onsen, kaiseki dinner) without sacrificing practicality
- Vegetarian shojin ryori available (48h notice) — the right answer for Jordan
- Elevator to all floors + ground-floor rooms available for Alex's knee
- English-speaking owner + staff speak basics (not fluent, but workable)
- Rooftop onsen with temple-district views
- Price: ~$220–260/night — reasonable for the character level
- Location: 5-min walk to Kiyomizu-dera, near Sannenzaka; walkable to multiple temples

**The catch:** Small property (17 rooms) in a popular district. Books 8–10 weeks ahead in autumn. You'd need to confirm availability and vegetarian menu details via email BEFORE committing.

---

### Strong Alternative: Arashiyama Yoshimura
**What it is:** Renovated traditional machiya (wooden townhouse), 8 rooms, ultra-authentic boutique inn.

**Why consider it:**
- Quieter neighborhood than Higashiyama — Arashiyama has fewer crowds and a slower pace
- Immediate access to bamboo grove (landmark photogenic moment for Alex) and Tenryu-ji temple
- Machiya character is *real* — low ceilings, wood beams, courtyard feel
- Vegetarian breakfast + shojin dinner by request
- Price: ~$190–220/night — slightly lower than Yumoto
- 10-day free cancellation

**The trade-offs:**
- Machiya = stairs are inevitable; confirm room layout before booking (may not be knee-friendly everywhere)
- Shared onsen (small, intimate) rather than private
- Fewer English speakers than city-center hotels; slower service during quiet seasons
- More "authentic" can mean less convenient (narrow corridors, creaky floors)

**Best for:** Jordan's temple-garden interests (immediate location) + love of quiet. Less ideal for Alex if knee fatigue is significant by evening.

---

### Practical Backup: Mitsui Garden Hotel Kyoto Sanjo
**What it is:** 4-star contemporary hotel, central Kyoto, business-luxury style.

**Why if the ryokans book out:**
- Excellent English support (staff trained for business travel)
- Modern elevator-accessible design; ground floor or accessible rooms standard
- Central location: 8-min walk to Nijo Castle, ~20-min to Higashiyama temples
- Vegetarian breakfast options; in-house restaurant
- Price: ~$123–165/night — lower than ryokans
- 14-day free cancellation

**The trade-off:** Zero traditional character. It's a well-run business hotel dressed up with Kyoto design nods. Practical, not cultural.

---

### Runner-up: Gracery Hotel (if truly budget-conscious)
**What it is:** 3.5-star contemporary, same neighborhood as Mitsui, more budget-friendly.

**Why:** Same practicality as Mitsui but ~$40/night cheaper. Best option if budget becomes a real constraint.

**The catch:** Even less character than Mitsui; feels generic.

---

### Luxury Splurge (if you want one special night): Ritz-Carlton Kyoto
**What it is:** Ultra-luxury modern ryokan hybrid, riverside Higashiyama, 39 suites.

**Why:** Michelin-starred kaiseki with vegetarian tasting menus, private onsen suites, ground-floor access, English concierge, photo-friendly architecture. Maximum comfort and English support for Alex.

**The catch:** ~$1700–2400/night. Over budget if this is the whole stay, but could work as an upgrade for a single night (e.g., arrival or special dinner).
