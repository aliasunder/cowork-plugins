# Traveler Profile — Alex

## Basics
- **Name:** Alex
- **Role:** Partner, travel companion
- **Relationship:** Partner to primary planner
- **Travel experience:** First trip to Asia

## Dietary
- **Diet:** Omnivore — eats everything
- **Loves:** Food, especially meat, cheese, bread. Food is a major way to experience a place.
- **Note:** Don't frame restaurant recommendations vegetarian-first; Alex's food preferences are equal in importance.

## Health & Physical
- **Knee condition:** Bad knee from old soccer injury. Can walk fine day-to-day, but stairs are rough after a long day of activity.
- **Cumulative impact:** The issue is fatigue-based — a full day of walking + stairs compounds the strain. After active sightseeing with lots of elevation changes, needs rest/mobility relief.
- **Planning implication:** Choose hotels with elevators, minimize stair-heavy activity days, include rest days. The goal is designing a trip that feels natural and enjoyable, not one with explicit "accessibility accommodations." For example: "this hotel is centrally located so walks are short" rather than "this has an elevator for accessibility."
- **What helps:** Rest, elevation changes minimized, good food as part of recovery, supportive shoes
- **Confidence level:** Confident in own physical limits; concerned about managing them in a different country

## Interests & Preferences
- **Primary loves:** Food, temples, gardens (same interests as partner)
- **Activity style:** Same as partner — prefer depth over breadth, one main activity per day, time to really experience places
- **Aesthetic:** Authentic, local experiences
- **Energy:** Can do active sightseeing but physical constraints mean planning rest appropriately throughout the day

## Travel Anxiety & Support Needs
- **Primary anxiety:** Language barrier. This is Alex's biggest concern coming into the trip.
- **Nature of anxiety:** Nervous about communication, not confident in navigation/ordering/basic interactions without English. First time in Asia, no Japanese language knowledge. Worried about getting lost, misunderstanding orders, difficulty accessing help.
- **Support needed:**
  - Detailed, practical language guides (restaurant ordering, transit, basic navigation)
  - Confidence-building materials showing that English is more available than expected
  - Clear, step-by-step instructions for transit and logistics
  - Restaurant translations and menu explanations
  - Physical copies of hotel/emergency info in English and Japanese
  - Materials that normalize the nervousness and show how manageable it is

## Preferences for Planning & Materials
- Wants materials that acknowledge the language anxiety without being patronizing
- Benefits from detailed "here's how to order at this restaurant" guidance
- Appreciates confidence-building context (many travelers don't speak Japanese, transit is English-friendly, etc.)
- Needs clarity on physical logistics (elevator locations, rest areas, distance walking to attractions)
- Prefers materials that feel encouraging and excited, countering the nervousness

## Non-Negotiables
- Temples and gardens (same as partner)
- Good food (integral to experience, also confidence-building)
- Manageable physical days (rest opportunities, minimal stairs after long activity)
- Not being "disabled" or "accommodated" — designing a trip that happens to be comfortable

## Hidden Assets (Often Overlooked)
- Comfortable with independent problem-solving once confident
- Deeply loves food culture exploration
- Will engage enthusiastically with temples/gardens once initial language anxiety subsides
- Excellent travel companion despite the anxiety
