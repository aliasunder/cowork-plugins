**Rationale:** Balances you and Alex's needs: you want good value, Alex benefits from predictability and flexibility. Allows for refundable hotels (reduces mobility/comfort surprises) without premium costs.

### Route Preliminary
**Decision:** Tentative route: Tokyo (3N) → Kyoto (4N) → Osaka (2N).

**Rationale:** Tokyo required as major hub. Kyoto is essential for temples/gardens (famous for both). ### Hotel Strategy
**Decision:** 3–4★, central location, elevator priority, local character, direct booking, free cancellation 7+ days.

**Rationale:** Meets comfort needs (central for short walks, elevator for Alex's mobility), supports local business, easier cancellation flexibility.

### Language Support Integration
**Decision:** All materials will include detailed restaurant translations and transit instructions to address Alex's language anxiety directly.

**Rationale:** Language barrier is Alex's primary anxiety. Detailed, confidence-building materials (not patronizing) are essential for trip enjoyment. This shapes all Phase 6 (restaurants) and Phase 7 (materials).

### Alex's Mobility Planning

**Rationale:** Per Alex's profile: "not disabled" framing. Designing a trip that happens to be comfortable (short walks, good hotels, rest days) is the approach. Materials will never mention accommodations; they'll frame every choice as aesthetically/experientially good (e.g., "this neighborhood is walkable and full of small shops" not "elevator").
