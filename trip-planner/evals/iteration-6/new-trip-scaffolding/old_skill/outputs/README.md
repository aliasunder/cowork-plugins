## Important Design Decisions

### 1. Alex's Mobility
**Approach:** Comfort-centered, not labeled as "accessibility."
- Hotels with elevators (natural good-location choice, not accommodation)
- Activity days designed around rest periods
- Materials never mention Alex's knee; they frame every choice as aesthetic/experiential

### 2. Alex's Language Anxiety
**Approach:** Confidence-building through detailed support.
- All restaurants will include Japanese translations for ordering
- Transit instructions will be step-by-step clarity
- Language cheat sheet will normalize the nervousness
- Materials warm and encouraging, not patronizing

### 3. Dietary Preferences
**Approach:** Balanced, not vegetarian-first.
- You vegetarian, Alex omnivore — both preferences equal
- Restaurant research will serve both equally
- Food as a primary joy, not reduced to dietary labels

### 4. Booking Philosophy
**Approach:** Balanced flexibility without overpaying.
- Refundable hotels prioritized (reduces surprises)
- Direct hotel booking (better rates, easier changes)
- United miles only if value >1.5 cpp (cents per point)
- Willing to pay for comfort (good location, no stairs fatigue) without premium excess
