## Key Planning Decisions Embedded

### 1. Alex's Mobility & Comfort
**Design principle:** Comfort-centered, not accessibility-labeled.
- Choose hotels with elevators (natural choice, not accommodation)
- Fewer stairs between hotel and activities
- Rest days built into schedule based on activity load
- Alex's walking capacity to be confirmed (Phase 1 wrap-up): How many consecutive full days? Rest threshold?
- **Never frame in traveler materials** as "accommodations for Alex's knee" — frame everything as aesthetic/experiential choices

### 2. Alex's Language Anxiety
**Design principle:** Confidence-building through detailed support.
- All restaurants will include detailed Japanese translations for ordering
- Transit instructions will be step-by-step clarity
- Language cheat sheet will include confidence-building context (e.g., "many travelers don't speak Japanese; transit is English-friendly; here's exactly what to say")
- Materials will be warm and encouraging, not patronizing
- This shapes all Phase 6 restaurant research and Phase 7 material production

### 3. Dietary: Balanced, Not Vegetarian-First
**Design principle:** Both travelers' food preferences matter equally.
- You are vegetarian (eggs/dairy OK); Alex is omnivore
- Restaurant research will NOT be framed vegetarian-first
- Both will have meal options that match their preferences
- Food is a primary joy of the trip; don't reduce it to dietary labels

### 4. Booking Strategy: United Miles Calculation
**Rule:** Only use miles if value >1.5 cents per point (cpp).
- Formula: (cash price in cents) ÷ (miles used) × 100 = cpp
- Example: Flight normally $1,200 (120,000 cents). Using 85K miles: 120,000 ÷ 85,000 × 100 = 1.41 cpp (not good enough, use cash instead)
- If miles give >1.5 cpp, use them. Otherwise, book cheaper cash option and save miles.

### 5. Hotel Philosophy: Direct Booking
**Policy:** Book hotels on their direct websites, not OTAs.
- Direct often has better rates, easier changes, better support
- Matches your established practice
