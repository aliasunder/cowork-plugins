# Restaurant Research — Kyoto

**Research Date:** April 2, 2026
**Trip Dates:** Oct 18-21, 2026 (4 days in Kyoto)

## Research Scope & Criteria

**Travelers:**
- Jordan: Strictly vegetarian (no fish sauce, no dashi with bonito, no animal-derived ingredients)
- Alex: Omnivore, appreciates diverse options, Japanese cuisine experience

**Categories Researched:**
1. **High-End Temple Cuisine (Shojin Ryori)** — Buddhist vegetarian tradition, perfect for Jordan
2. **Casual Vegetarian Spots** — Ramen, cafes, noodles (accessible, less formal)
3. **Traditional Kyoto Restaurants with Vegetarian Options** — Gion area, neighborhood exploration
4. **Market & Food Tour Options** — Nishiki Market (rain alternative in itinerary)
5. **Arashiyama Area** — Near Day 4 activities (bamboo grove, Tenryu-ji)

**Hard Filters:**
- Must safely accommodate strict vegetarian (no hidden fish stocks/bonito)
- Must be reasonably accessible to non-Japanese speakers (English menu, obvious cuisine, staff support)
- Rating ≥4.5/5 or 8.5+/10 across sources (Michelin, Tripadvisor, Google)

**Nice-to-Haves:**
- Local/non-touristy where feasible
- Booking flexibility (walk-in acceptable)
- Day-of-week/seasonal adjustments noted
- Price range fits relaxed budget

**Quality Target:** 4.5+ rating, Michelin-recognized where available

## Phase 3: Cross-Validation & Details

### Tier 1: High-End (Michelin/Featured)

**SHIGETSU (Tenryu-ji Temple, Arashiyama)**
- **Rating:** Michelin Bib Gourmand, ~4.7/5 across sources
- **Type:** Zen Buddhist shojin ryori (vegetarian cuisine)
- **Hours:** 11:00 am – 2:00 pm daily (closed Thursdays)
- **Reservation:** Required for 2+ guests, minimum 3 days advance
- **Phone:** (075) 882-9725 (9am-5pm)
- **Address:** 68 Susukinobaba-cho, Saga-Tenryu-ji, Ukyo-ku
- **Price:** Yen (approx. $25-35 USD per person)
- **Garden Admission:** ¥500 additional
- **Dietary:** Fully vegetarian, can accommodate strict vegan
- **Accessibility:** Counter seating, within temple grounds
- **Why It Works:** Jordan's primary pick for temple cuisine. Same-day activity is Arashiyama/Tenryu-ji (Oct 21) — **perfect co-location**. Alex will appreciate the cultural depth even though omnivore menu unavailable. Advance booking required but worth the planning.
- **Risk:** 3-day booking window requires clarity on dates; temple admission fee adds to total
- **Recent Reviews:** Praised for artful presentation, seasonal vegetables, cultural experience

**AJIRO HONTEN (Myōshin-ji Temple, Kita-ku)**
- **Rating:** Michelin-recognized, ~4.6/5, longest-running vegan restaurant with Michelin star
- **Type:** Shojin ryori (Buddhist vegetarian)
- **Hours:** Lunch 12pm-3pm | Dinner 5pm-9pm (enter by 7pm), closed Wednesdays
- **Reservation:** Recommended for dinner
- **Price:** Lunch ¥3,000 (~$20) | Dinner ¥6,000+ (~$40+)
- **Address:** Just south of Myōshin-ji Temple gates, Kita-ku
- **Dietary:** No meat, fish, or dairy; fully vegan-friendly
- **Why It Works:** Different from Shigetsu (more dinner-forward, different temple). Seasonal vegetables heavy. Chef worked in temple kitchen. High prestige.
- **Logistics:** Located in Kita (north Kyoto), may be less convenient depending on ryokan location. Best for Oct 19-20 flexible days.
- **Recent Reviews:** Praised for beauty of presentation, seasonal ingredients, spiritual approach

**LITTLE HEAVEN (Arashiyama, outer)**
- **Rating:** ~4.5/5, modern creative vegan shojin
- **Type:** Modern reinterpretation of shojin ryori, mix of traditional + Western vegan
- **Booking:** 3+ weeks advance recommended (often booked out)
- **Price:** Course-based (approx. $40-60 USD)
- **Why It Works:** Modern take on temple food for a lighter meal. Creative presentation appeals to both travelers.
- **Logistics:** Few train stops from Arashiyama center — may be a trip within the trip
- **Risk:** Requires very early booking; less authentic than traditional shojin

### Tier 2: Accessible Casual (Walk-in/Easy Booking)

**GION TANTO (Gion)**
- **Rating:** 4.5/5, often has queues
- **Type:** Okonomiyaki (Japanese savory pancake) — fully vegan menu available
- **Style:** Traditional Kyoto okonomiyaki, vegan options extensive
- **Hours:** Open daily (confirm exact hours)
- **Booking:** Walk-in acceptable (expect waits at peak times)
- **Price:** ~¥1,500-2,000 (~$10-15 USD)
- **Address:** Gion district (exact address TBD)
- **Dietary:** Separate vegan menu, very accommodating
- **Why It Works:** Very close to Day 2-3 activities (Nanzen-ji/Gion evening exploration). Casual, no reservation pressure. Jordan gets dedicated vegan menu. Alex enjoys traditional Japanese.
- **Logistics:** Gion is walking distance from multiple planned activities
- **Recent Reviews:** Praised for vegan menu quality, authentic flavor, generous portions

**UZU (Vegan Ramen)**
- **Rating:** 4.6/5, darkened room with teamLab art
- **Type:** Vegan ramen (flavorful broth, fresh toppings)
- **Booking:** Advance booking several weeks in high season
- **Price:** ~¥1,200-1,500 (~$10-12 USD)
- **Dietary:** Fully vegan ramen
- **Why It Works:** Unique experience (darkened room, art), perfect for a quick lunch. Strong vegetarian credentials.
- **Risk:** Requires advance booking (less flexible for day-of)
- **Recent Reviews:** Praised for broth quality despite being plant-based, artistic ambiance

**NISHIKI MARKET FOOD TOUR (Central Kyoto)**
- **Rating:** 4.3/5 (tour operators vary)
- **Type:** Guided food tour of 400-year-old market
- **Duration:** 1.5-3 hours depending on tour
- **Price:** ~$50-80 USD per person (varies by operator)
- **Dietary:** Vegetarian options available with advance notice; notify guide
- **Booking:** Most tours allow walk-in but advance booking recommended
- **Why It Works:** **Rain alternative** from itinerary for Oct 20 (Nanzen-ji). Covered market, educational, good for both travelers. Vegetarian customization available.
- **Recent Reviews:** Praised for local flavors, knowledgeable guides, good for diverse diets with notice

**MUSUBI CAFE ARASHIYAMA (Arashiyama)**
- **Rating:** 4.4/5, vegetarian cafe
- **Type:** Whole-grain vegetarian cafe
- **Location:** 3-min walk from Hankyu Arashiyama Station
- **Hours:** Lunch service (confirm times)
- **Price:** ~¥1,200-1,500 (~$10-12 USD)
- **Menu:** Today's Special Lunch A/B, chickpea curry, vegetable pasta, vegan options
- **Booking:** Walk-in acceptable
- **Why It Works:** Day 4 (Oct 21) Arashiyama activities — same-location dining. Light, healthy option. Full vegan menu available.
- **Recent Reviews:** Praised for local Kyoto vegetables, lunch variety, vegan accommodation

### Tier 3: Specialized/Niche

**MIMIKOU (Udon, Yasaka Shrine area)**
- **Rating:** 4.1/5
- **Type:** Traditional udon noodle shop
- **Menu:** Two pages dedicated to vegetarian dishes (curry udon especially praised)
- **Booking:** Walk-in
- **Price:** ~¥800-1,200 (~$6-10 USD)

**TOSUIRO (Tofu Cuisine, Kyoto)**
- **Rating:** 4.3/5
- **Type:** Tofu elevated to art form
- **Booking:** Likely advance booking
- **Price:** Mid-to-high range
- **Why It Works:** Specialized tofu expertise, entirely suitable for Jordan. Unique experience.

**DAITOKUJI IKKYU (Daitokuji Temple, Kita)**
- **Rating:** 4.4/5
- **Type:** Shojin ryori (oldest continuous Buddhist vegetarian restaurant in Kyoto, 500+ years)
- **Historical Significance:** Highest prestige tier among Buddhist restaurants
- **Price:** Likely mid-to-high range
- **Booking:** Advance booking likely required
- **Why It Works:** Unmatched historical prestige. Alternative to Ajiro if that fills. Kita location noted.
