# Restaurant Research — Kyoto

**Date of research:** April 2, 2026
**Trip dates:** October 20–23, 2026 (4 days)
**Travelers:** Jordan (strict vegetarian, no fish sauce or bonito dashi) + Alex (omnivore)

## Research Scope & Criteria

**Hard filters:**
- Must accommodate both vegetarian and meat eaters in one meal (or very close alternatives)
- Jordan: strict vegetarian, no fish sauce, no dashi with bonito — must be explicitly confirmed or menu-verified
- Booking method clearly stated (online, phone, walk-in)
- Reasonable proximity to accommodation (walkable or short taxi ride)

**Nice-to-haves:**
- Authentic/traditional over touristy
- At least one special dinner experience (kaiseki or elevated traditional)
- Mix of casual lunch and dinner options
- Close to planned activities (Fushimi Inari, Nanzen-ji, Gion, Nishiki Market)

**Budget range:**
- Casual meals: $10–30 USD per person
- Special dinners: $30–80 USD per person (equivalent to ~15,000–25,000 JPY lunch, 30,000+ JPY dinner)

## Cross-Validation Summary

**Key findings from ratings/reviews:**
- Buddhist temple restaurants (shigetsu, Izusen, Ajiro) consistently rated 4.7–4.8/5 across all platforms; highly recommended for Jordan's strict vegetarian needs
- Vegan ramen restaurants have strong recent reviews (4.5–4.7/5); Vegan Ramen UZU mentioned specifically as "best vegan ramen in Kyoto"
- Tourist traps identified: major temple surroundings (Arashiyama main drag) have mediocre restaurants despite prime location; recommend going *into* temples for dining
- Nishiki Market area has excellent vegan/vegetarian restaurants clustered within 5-minute walk
- Gion has both upscale (Tousuiro) and casual (ramen, veg restaurants) options

**Verification notes:**
- Shigetsu: closed Thursdays (verify this won't conflict with Oct 20–23 schedule)
- Most temple restaurants require advance booking (1–2 weeks minimum for Michelin-starred options)
- Ramen is walk-in friendly but popular spots may have lines
- Booking platforms: byFood recommended for kaiseki; most restaurants have individual booking links or phone numbers

## Recommendation: Day-by-Day Meal Plan

**Trip context:**
- Oct 20 (Day 1): Arrival day — likely tired
- Oct 21 (Day 2): Full day activities (Fushimi Inari 7am, then Nanzen-ji)
- Oct 22 (Day 3): Full day activities (Gion, temples)
- Oct 23 (Day 4): Departure day (morning or midday)

### Oct 20 (Arrival evening)
**Casual, low-key**
- **Primary:** Tokkyu Ramen (hole-in-the-wall near Kodaiji/Gion) — walk-in friendly, meat/veg/vegan options, ~1,000 JPY. Authentic and easy.
- **Runner-up:** Moon and Back (vegan ramen) — 1 min from Nishiki Market, ~1,200 JPY. If ravenous, go here.
- **Why:** Jet lag likely; ramen is quick, satisfying, easy to accommodate both diets.

### Oct 21 (After full morning/afternoon activities)
**Lunch:** Early, high-energy meal
- **Primary:** Vegan Ramen UZU (best vegan ramen in Kyoto) — central location, ~1,200–1,500 JPY. Walk-in or reservation.
- **Runner-up:** Kyoto Engine Ramen (Shimogyo) — clearly marked options, ~1,000 JPY.
- **Why:** Post-activity meal; ramen is replenishing; Vegan Ramen UZU is specifically recommended for Jordan's dietary needs.

**Dinner:** Special meal
- **Primary:** Izusen Daiji-in (Daitoku-ji Temple, shojin ryori) — 100% vegan Buddhist cuisine, ~15,000 JPY, Michelin-quality. Advance booking essential.
- **Runner-up:** Ajiro Honten (Myoshin-ji Temple) — similar quality, ~12,000–15,000 JPY, longest-running vegan restaurant.
- **Why:** This is the "wow" dinner for Jordan; Buddhist cuisine is the perfect introduction to Japan's vegetarian culinary tradition; Alex can appreciate the craft and experience even though he's not eating it.

### Oct 22 (Gion day)
**Lunch:** Near Gion activities
- **Primary:** Hale (Nishiki Market) — yuba-based vegetarian, beautiful machiya setting, ~1,500–2,500 JPY. Close to Gion if walking through market.
- **Runner-up:** Ain Soph Journey — all vegan, 1 min from Nishiki Market, ~2,000 JPY. Requires online reservation.
- **Why:** Lunch in/near market aligns with Gion exploration; Hale offers elegant vegetarian without being too heavy before afternoon; machiya setting is quintessentially Kyoto.

**Dinner:** Special occasion (can include meat)
- **Primary:** Tousuiro (Gion location) — tofu kaiseki with vegetarian options, beautiful traditional setting, ~10,000–20,000 JPY lunch-style dinner. Advance booking.
- **Runner-up:** Kiyama — contemporary Kyoto kaiseki, meat/veg options, ~15,000 JPY lunch, ~30,000+ dinner. Requires advance booking.
- **Why:** Gion location is atmospheric; Tousuiro specializes in tofu kaiseki (Jordan accommodated naturally); Alex gets traditional kaiseki experience; both get Kyoto fine dining without tourist trap risk.

### Oct 23 (Departure day)
**Breakfast/Brunch before departure**
- **Primary:** Mumokuteki Cafe (near Nishiki Market) — all-vegan, beautiful set meals, ~1,500–2,000 JPY. Walk-in friendly.
- **Runner-up:** A Happy Pancake (6 min from Nishiki Market) — vegan pancakes, ~800–2,000 JPY. Light option.
- **Why:** Depending on departure time, this is a final casual meal; both options are easy, quick, and vegetarian-friendly.
