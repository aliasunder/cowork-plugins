## Key Decisions

**Research Scope:** Focused on dietary hard filter (strict vegetarian for Jordan, omnivore for Alex) as primary organizing principle. This shaped search strategy: started with all-vegan options, then added mixed-diet restaurants with strong vegetarian accommodations.

**Recommendation Structure:** Presented recommendations in day-by-day context (Oct 20–23 schedule) rather than ranked list, directly aligned with activity schedule (Fushimi Inari morning, Gion day, departure). This makes meal choices tangible and reveals natural pairing opportunities (ramen after early-morning temples, kaiseki in Gion evening).

**Buddhist Temple Restaurants:** Identified Izusen Daiji-in and Ajiro Honten as signature "wow" experiences — Michelin-starred, 4.7–4.8/5 ratings across all sources, 100% vegan Buddhist shojin ryori. Cross-validated across TripAdvisor, Tabelog, Inside Kyoto, Michelin Guide. These are the only restaurants in the research that exclusively accommodate Jordan's strict vegetarian needs; they require 1–2 week advance booking.

**Vegan Ramen:** Vegan Ramen UZU flagged as "best vegan ramen in Kyoto" across 3+ independent sources; verified soy milk broth (not bonito dashi), accommodates Jordan's no-fish-products requirement, walk-in or reservation at ~1,200 JPY.

**Tourist Trap Detection:** Identified high-traffic zones (Arashiyama main, immediate temple surroundings) as mediocre despite prime location; recommended dining *inside* temples instead (Izusen inside Daitoku-ji, Ajiro near Myoshin-ji).
